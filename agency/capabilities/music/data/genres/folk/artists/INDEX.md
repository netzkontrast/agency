## Reference Tracks

### Israel Kamakawiwoʻole (IZ)
- "Over the Rainbow/What a Wonderful World" (Facing Future, 1993) -- Solo ukulele and voice, single take at 3 AM; 1B+ YouTube views; the most famous Hawaiian recording ever
- "Hawaiʻi '78" (Facing Future, 1993) -- Hawaiian sovereignty anthem; imagines royalty weeping at modern Hawaii; politically powerful
- "E Ala Ē" (E Ala Ē, 1995) -- Call to political awakening for Native Hawaiians; uptempo, urgent, warm
- "White Sandy Beach of Hawaiʻi" (Facing Future, 1993) -- Classic Hawaiian standard; gentle ukulele, sweet vocals, pure island nostalgia
- "Kaulana Kawaihae" (Facing Future, 1993) -- Traditional Hawaiian song; falsetto vocal tradition showcase
- "Somewhere Over the Rainbow" (Alone in IZ World, 2001) -- Alternate solo version; more intimate and raw than the medley
- "Take Me Home, Country Roads" (Alone in IZ World, 2001) -- John Denver cover as Hawaiian ukulele ballad; transforms source material completely
- "Maui Hawaiian Sup'pa Man" (Facing Future, 1993) -- Uptempo, joyful, full-band; reggae-Hawaiian fusion; celebratory island energy

### Mumford & Sons
- "Little Lion Man" (Sigh No More, 2009) — Breakthrough single; aggressive banjo, quiet-to-explosive dynamics; #1 Alternative Songs
- "The Cave" (Sigh No More, 2009) — Anthemic folk rock; Plato's cave allegory; four Grammy nominations
- "I Will Wait" (Babel, 2012) — Biggest single (#12 Hot 100); quintessential quiet-to-loud dynamic arc
- "Babel" (Babel, 2012) — Title track of Grammy AOTY; biblical imagery; full-band acoustic assault
- "White Blank Page" (Sigh No More, 2009) — Solo banjo to full-band catharsis; extreme dynamic range
- "Believe" (Wilder Mind, 2015) — Electric reinvention; no banjo; synths and atmospheric production
- "Guiding Light" (Delta, 2018) — Middle-ground sound; acoustic-electric blend; arena-folk
- "Rushmere" (Rushmere, 2025) — Trio-era return to roots; folk-pop with banjo and anthemic peak
