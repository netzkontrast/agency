# Wave

## Genre Overview

Wave emerged around 2016 from the SoundCloud underground as a dark, atmospheric hybrid of trap, grime, and ambient electronic music. The genre crystallized when producers like Kareful, Klimeks, and Sorsari began crafting tracks that combined trap's half-time rhythms and 808 bass with ethereal synth pads, drenched-in-reverb atmospheres, and pitched vocal samples that evoked both UK garage's spectral quality and shoegaze's wall-of-sound immersion. The term "wave" was coined by the community itself, describing music that felt like being submerged in dark, undulating sound.

The genre's sonic identity is defined by its melancholic, nocturnal atmosphere. Where trap aimed for aggression and EDM aimed for euphoria, wave pursued a more introspective emotional register — loneliness, longing, urban isolation, and the particular sadness of late-night internet browsing. Production techniques center on heavy reverb, lo-fi textures, pitch-shifted vocal samples (often R&B or pop fragments), detuned synths, and sparse but impactful 808 patterns. The result is music that feels simultaneously intimate and vast, like hearing a distant transmission through fog.

Wave's cultural home was SoundCloud, where the genre developed its own ecosystem of producers, labels (Liquid Ritual, Aerobics), and visual aesthetics — dark, desaturated imagery of empty urban spaces, anime screenshots, and vaporwave-adjacent graphics. The genre spread through online communities, DJ mixes, and events like Wavemob nights in London. By the early 2020s, wave had influenced mainstream electronic production, with its atmospheric techniques appearing in trap, dubstep, and pop. Artists like Skeler brought wave to larger audiences through YouTube mixes that soundtracked millions of late-night listening sessions. The genre continues to evolve, with newer producers incorporating elements of ambient, UK bass, and even post-rock while maintaining wave's core emotional identity.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Detuned synth pads with heavy reverb, 808 sub-bass and kicks, sparse trap hi-hats and snares, pitched vocal samples (often R&B or pop fragments), atmospheric textures (rain, static, field recordings), minimal melodic elements (single-note synth lines, plucked sounds), grime-influenced bass stabs; everything processed through layers of reverb and delay

- **Vocals**: Pitched and processed vocal samples (not original vocals in most productions); fragments of R&B, pop, or spoken word drenched in reverb and delay; ghostly, spectral quality; sometimes chopped rhythmically; when original vocals appear, they are typically whispered, pitched down, or heavily effected; gender-ambiguous through processing

- **Production**: Heavy reverb as defining characteristic (long tails, shimmer reverbs), lo-fi textures and tape hiss, half-time drum patterns, sparse arrangement with negative space as compositional tool, sidechain compression creating a breathing quality, detuned oscillators, stereo width manipulation, low-pass filtering for warmth, subtle distortion on synths; mastering tends toward dark, warm tonality rather than brightness

- **Energy/Mood**: Melancholic, nocturnal, introspective, lonely; urban isolation and late-night emotional states; wistful and longing; simultaneously calming and deeply sad; dark but not aggressive; atmospheric immersion; the emotional register of 3 AM in a city

- **Structure**: Extended intros with atmospheric buildup, verse-drop structure borrowed from trap but with gentler transitions, drops that hit emotionally rather than aggressively, breakdowns that strip back to ambient pads, 808 patterns entering gradually; tracks often 3-5 minutes with patient development; some tracks are essentially ambient with rhythmic elements

- **Tempo**: 130-160 BPM in half-time (effectively 65-80 BPM feel)

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: Free or ABCB — loose structures that match the genre's atmospheric, impressionistic quality
- **Rhyme quality**: Soft, breathy, impressionistic; near rhymes and assonance preferred over hard rhymes; words chosen for texture as much as meaning
- **Verse structure**: 2-4 lines per verse; sparse and evocative; negative space between phrases mirrors the production style
- **Key rule**: Less is more. Lyrics should feel like fragments — overheard whispers, half-remembered thoughts, emotional impressions. Write for reverb-drenched, pitched delivery. Atmosphere over narrative.
- **Avoid**: Dense lyrical passages; upbeat or energetic language; complex rhyme schemes; anything that fights the melancholic, spacious atmosphere; aggressive delivery
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **2-3 lines/verse** at 130-160 BPM half-time. Sparse, spacious phrasing. Leave room for reverb tails and atmospheric elements. Topics: 1/track. Emotional impressionism over storytelling. Whispered or soft delivery.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Classic Wave** | The core sound: dark trap-influenced beats with ethereal atmospheres, pitched vocals, and melancholic mood; SoundCloud-native | Kareful, Klimeks, Sorsari |
| **Hardwave** | More aggressive variant with heavier bass, grime influences, and harder-hitting drums; maintains atmosphere but adds intensity | Deadcrow, Juuku, Ytho |
| **Ambient Wave** | Stripped-back variant emphasizing atmosphere over rhythm; closer to ambient music with occasional trap elements; deeply immersive | Brothel, Vacant, Cold Mega |
| **Vaporwave-Wave Hybrid** | Blends wave's dark atmospheres with vaporwave's nostalgic, sample-based aesthetic; lo-fi and dreamlike | Various SoundCloud producers |
| **Future Garage Wave** | Incorporates UK garage rhythms and future garage's shuffled beats into wave's atmospheric framework; more rhythmically complex | Skeler, Klimeks (some works) |
| **Phonk-Wave Hybrid** | Darker variant incorporating Memphis rap samples, cowbells, and phonk's distorted aesthetics into wave's atmospheric palette | Various producers at the genre boundary |

## Reference Tracks

- **Kareful - "Bliss"** — Genre-defining track: sparse 808 patterns under cascading reverb-drenched synths and pitched vocal fragments; captures the exact emotional frequency that wave occupies — melancholic, beautiful, and deeply lonely

- **Klimeks - "It's Already Done"** — Emotive wave at its peak: the pitched vocal sample carries genuine grief while the production wraps it in atmospheric warmth; demonstrates how wave transforms sampled fragments into something deeply personal

- **Skeler - "Tel Aviv"** — Accessible wave: melodic enough for casual listeners while maintaining the genre's atmospheric depth; YouTube mix staple that introduced millions to the sound; demonstrates wave's crossover potential

- **Sorsari - "Hounds"** — Dark, immersive wave: minimal elements creating maximum atmosphere; the 808 hits feel like heartbeats in an empty room; exemplifies the genre's "less is more" philosophy

- **Brothel - "404"** — Ambient wave landmark: barely-there rhythms under vast atmospheric pads; pushes wave toward pure ambient while retaining its emotional identity; music for 3 AM contemplation

- **Deadcrow - "Search"** — Hardwave essential: maintains wave's atmosphere while adding grime-influenced bass weight and harder-hitting drums; proves the genre can be both delicate and powerful simultaneously
