# PC Music

## Genre Overview

PC Music is both a record label and an aesthetic movement founded by A.G. Cook in London in 2013. The label became the nucleus of a radical reimagining of pop music — one that treated commercial pop's glossy surfaces, Auto-Tuned vocals, and synthetic textures not as guilty pleasures but as legitimate artistic material worthy of deconstruction, exaggeration, and critical celebration. What emerged was a sound that was simultaneously hyper-synthetic and deeply emotional, ironic and sincere, futuristic and nostalgic — a post-internet pop that reflected a generation raised on both bubblegum and bandwidth.

The PC Music aesthetic is defined by its hypergloss production: vocals pitch-shifted to doll-like extremes, synths polished to a plastic sheen, and arrangements that push pop conventions past the point of parody into something genuinely new. SOPHIE was the movement's most visionary producer, creating textures that sounded like inflatable plastics and liquid metals — sounds that had never existed before. Hannah Diamond brought genuine emotional depth to the crystalline production. Danny L Harle channeled happy hardcore's euphoria into postmodern pop songs. The collective demonstrated that artificiality, pushed far enough, becomes its own form of authenticity.

PC Music's influence on mainstream pop has been seismic despite the label's relatively small catalog. Charli XCX became the movement's most visible ambassador, bringing A.G. Cook's production to festival stages and Billboard charts. The aesthetic directly spawned hyperpop as a broader genre category and reshaped how pop producers approach vocal processing, texture design, and the tension between polish and experimentation. Following SOPHIE's death in 2021 and A.G. Cook's announcement that the label would wind down active releases, PC Music's legacy shifted from active movement to foundational influence — its ideas now so thoroughly absorbed into pop's mainstream that its once-radical experiments sound prescient rather than provocative.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Hyper-polished digital synthesizers, elastic and metallic bass textures (SOPHIE's signature), bright supersaw pads, trance leads, pitch-shifted vocal chops, bubblegum synth textures, plastic-sounding percussion, 808 kicks processed to extremes, MIDI-perfect arpeggios; everything sounds deliberately synthetic and pristine

- **Vocals**: Pitch-shifted (often upward to doll-like or robotic registers), Auto-Tuned as aesthetic choice, formant-shifted, layered in precise harmonies, breathy and intimate delivery juxtaposed with processed extremes; gender ambiguity through processing; vocals treated as another synthetic texture in the mix

- **Production**: Hypercompressed and ultra-bright mastering, surgical precision in arrangement, deliberate artificiality as core principle, sidechain pumping, trance gates, metallic and plastic textures that reference consumer products, minimalist arrangements with maximalist processing; every element polished to an uncanny sheen

- **Energy/Mood**: Post-ironic — simultaneously celebratory and critical of pop culture; euphoric but unsettling; sweet but with an uncanny valley quality; playful and conceptual; emotionally sincere beneath layers of artifice; themes of desire, identity, consumerism, and digital existence

- **Structure**: Pop song structures (verse-chorus-bridge) maintained but subverted; unexpected tempo or key shifts; breakdowns that deconstruct the song's own elements; hooks designed for maximum earworm potential; songs often shorter than radio standard (2-3 minutes)

- **Tempo**: 120-140 BPM (pop-standard range, occasionally dipping into ballad territory or pushing into trance speeds)

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: ABAB or AABB — pop-standard schemes maintained; the subversion is in delivery and production, not lyric structure
- **Rhyme quality**: Clean, precise, almost childlike simplicity; deceptively straightforward language that reveals depth on repeated listening
- **Verse structure**: 4 lines per verse; concise pop writing; hooks and choruses emphasized over verses
- **Key rule**: Lyrics should work on two levels — surface-level pop catchiness and deeper commentary on pop itself, consumerism, desire, or digital identity. Write for pitched-up, processed delivery.
- **Avoid**: Overly wordy or literary language; anything that fights the synthetic aesthetic; unprocessed singer-songwriter sincerity; complexity for its own sake
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **4 lines/verse** at 120-140 BPM. Pop-standard density. Short, hooky phrases that land clearly through vocal processing. Topics: 1/verse. Prioritize chorus memorability.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Hypergloss Pop** | The core PC Music sound: ultra-polished synthetic pop with pitch-shifted vocals and plastic textures; pop as both medium and critique | A.G. Cook, Hannah Diamond, Easyfun |
| **Deconstructed Pop** | More experimental end; takes pop structures apart and reassembles them into challenging new forms; art-gallery adjacent | SOPHIE, Arca (orbit), Lotic |
| **Bubblegum Bass** | Saccharine pop melodies over aggressive or distorted bass; sweet-and-sour contrast; candy-coated aggression | SOPHIE, A.G. Cook, QT |
| **Post-PC Music** | Mainstream pop informed by PC Music aesthetics; polished production with experimental edges brought to wider audiences | Charli XCX, Rina Sawayama, Kim Petras |
| **Harlecore** | Happy hardcore and trance filtered through PC Music's conceptual lens; rave nostalgia as pop art; euphoric and playful | Danny L Harle |
| **Conceptual Pop** | PC Music releases with strong visual/conceptual components; music as one element of a larger art project; brand parody | QT, GFOTY, A.G. Cook |

## Reference Tracks

- **SOPHIE - "Bipp"** — The founding document: rubbery bass, metallic clicks, and pitched vocal fragments over a deceptively simple pop structure; three minutes that launched an entire aesthetic movement; sounds like nothing before it

- **SOPHIE - "Immaterial"** — Ecstatic anthem of transcendence: impossibly processed vocals declaring freedom from physical form over euphoric synths; embodies PC Music's queer, post-physical ethos at its most moving

- **A.G. Cook - "Beautiful"** — Maximalist production thesis: layers of trance, pop, and noise collapsing into each other; demonstrates Cook's approach of overwhelming the listener with beauty and information simultaneously

- **Hannah Diamond - "Every Night"** — Emotional core of PC Music: genuine longing expressed through crystalline production; proves the aesthetic can achieve classic pop songwriting heights while remaining adventurous

- **Danny L Harle - "On a Mountain"** — Happy hardcore filtered through PC Music sensibility: euphoric vocals, trance arpeggios, pure joy; from the *Harlecore* virtual rave; shows the movement's debt to 90s rave culture

- **Charli XCX - "Vroom Vroom" (prod. SOPHIE)** — The crossover moment: Charli's pop star presence over SOPHIE's most aggressive production; revving synth bass became iconic; proved PC Music could work at arena scale

- **QT - "Hey QT"** — Pop song as conceptual art: fictional energy drink mascot singing an irresistibly catchy track produced by SOPHIE and A.G. Cook; the line between commerce and art deliberately erased

- **A.G. Cook - "Xxoplex"** — PC Music manifesto: warped samples, trance breakdowns, joyful chaos; demonstrates how the movement deconstructs and celebrates dance music simultaneously
