# Cloud Rap

## Genre Overview

Cloud rap is a subgenre of hip-hop that emerged in the late 2000s and early 2010s, built on hazy, reverb-drenched production that drew from ambient music, new age, chillwave, and trap. The term was coined in 2010 by music blogger Walker Chambliss (Walkmasterflex) of the Space Age Hustle blog to describe the music of Main Attrakionz member Squadda B. The genre's sonic foundation was largely established by New Jersey producer Clams Casino, whose ethereal instrumentals — most famously "I'm God" (2009), built on a stretched Imogen Heap sample for Lil B — became the template for the sound. Lil B's prolific, boundary-pushing output as the Based God, combined with Clams Casino's production and the Bay Area collective Main Attrakionz, formed the genre's earliest nucleus. The style prioritized atmosphere and mood over traditional hip-hop technicality, creating spacious, dreamlike tracks that felt weightless compared to the hard-hitting trap dominating Southern rap at the time.

The genre's reach expanded dramatically in 2011 when A$AP Rocky's debut singles "Peso" and "Purple Swag," along with his mixtape *Live.Love.A$AP* (produced in part by Clams Casino), brought cloud rap aesthetics to a mainstream audience. Rocky's fusion of Houston's chopped-and-screwed traditions with cloud rap's ethereal production and a fashion-forward visual identity proved the style could cross over without losing its atmospheric core. Simultaneously, across the Atlantic, a 16-year-old Swedish rapper named Yung Lean uploaded "Ginseng Strip 2002" to YouTube in 2013, and the track went viral. Lean and his Sad Boys collective (with producers Yung Sherman and Gud) alongside the closely affiliated Drain Gang crew (Bladee, Ecco2K, Thaiboy Digital, and producer Whitearmor) developed a distinctly European strain of cloud rap that leaned into melancholy, irony, and internet culture. Their music layered Auto-Tuned vocals over glacial synths, video game samples, and trap percussion, establishing Stockholm as an unlikely second capital of the genre.

Cloud rap was inseparable from the internet era that produced it. The genre emerged alongside SoundCloud (2007), Bandcamp, and file-sharing platforms that allowed artists to bypass traditional gatekeepers entirely. Its visual identity drew from early internet aesthetics — Arizona Iced Tea cans, vaporwave imagery, Japanese characters, Windows 95 nostalgia, and lo-fi digital art — creating a complete audiovisual world that circulated through Tumblr, YouTube, and social media. Cloud rap's influence proved far-reaching: it laid direct groundwork for emo rap (Bones, Lil Peep), the digicore and hyperpop movements, and the atmospheric production that became standard across mainstream hip-hop. Collectives like Raider Klan (SpaceGhostPurrp, Denzel Curry), Seshollowaterboyz (Bones, Xavier Wulf, Chris Travis), and GothBoiClique (Lil Tracy, Wicca Phase Springs Eternal) all emerged from or alongside cloud rap's ecosystem. Though the genre's peak cultural moment has passed, its production techniques and internet-native distribution model permanently reshaped how hip-hop is made and consumed.

## Characteristics

- **Production**: Ethereal, reverb-heavy beats built on airy synth pads, pitch-shifted vocal samples, and ambient textures. Producers favor lush reverb and delay tails, detuned melodies, and short loopable phrases (2-4 bars). Samples are often drawn from J-pop, anime soundtracks, video game music, and new age recordings, chopped and processed into unrecognizable textures
- **Drums**: Trap-derived percussion (808 kicks, hi-hat rolls, crisp snares) but softer and more restrained than standard trap. 808 bass is present but sits within the mix rather than dominating it. Kick patterns are sparse, hi-hats are gentler, and the overall percussion feels laid-back rather than aggressive
- **Vocals**: Heavily processed with Auto-Tune, reverb, and delay. Delivery ranges from mumbled and detached to melodic and sing-song. Vocal tone tends toward emotionally flat or melancholic rather than energetic. Pitched-down or pitched-up vocal effects are common, as are layered vocal stacks that blur into the production
- **Tempo**: Typically 120-150 BPM on the grid, but the half-time drum programming creates an effective feel of 60-75 BPM. The slow, drifting pace is central to the genre's hypnotic quality
- **Mood**: Melancholic, introspective, and detached. Cloud rap occupies an emotional space between sadness and apathy, often evoking late-night solitude, drug-induced haze, or existential drift. The atmosphere is spacious and cold rather than warm or intimate
- **Structure**: Loose and repetitive. Verses and hooks blend together without sharp transitions. Songs often feel like extended loops with gradual layering rather than dramatic builds or drops. Intros and outros may stretch into ambient passages

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: Loose couplets (AABB) or free-form. Near rhymes and assonance favored over tight end rhymes. Many lines deliberately avoid rhyming altogether.
- **Rhyme quality**: Intentionally imprecise. Melodic cadence and vowel matching matter more than clean rhyme pairs. Slant rhymes and repetition-as-hook are standard.
- **Verse structure**: 8-12 lines per verse. Short lines with low syllable counts (5-8 per bar). Significant empty space between vocal phrases — the beat breathes.
- **Key rule**: Mood and atmosphere over meaning. Lyrics are impressionistic, not narrative. Concrete images (brand names, drug references, weather, consumer products) sit alongside abstract or surreal phrases without logical connective tissue.
- **Avoid**: Dense technical rapping, complex multisyllabic rhyme schemes, motivational or uplifting messaging, and conventional storytelling. The tone should never feel effortful or eager.
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **6 lines/verse** at 120-150 BPM (60-75 effective half-time). Sparse vocal placement with long gaps. Mumbled or melodic delivery. Repetitive hooks (2-4 word phrases looped). Topics: 1/verse max, often fragmented.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | BPM Range | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Classic Cloud Rap** | The foundational Bay Area sound: ethereal Clams Casino-style production, Based God-influenced delivery, atmospheric samples over trap drums. Lo-fi and raw, distributed through early SoundCloud and YouTube. | 120-140 | Lil B, Clams Casino, Main Attrakionz |
| **Sad Boys / Drain Gang** | Stockholm-rooted strain emphasizing melancholy, irony, and internet culture. Glacial synths, Auto-Tuned vocals, video game and anime samples. Emotionally detached delivery with a fashion-forward, meme-aware aesthetic. | 120-145 | Yung Lean, Bladee, Ecco2K, Thaiboy Digital |
| **A$AP Cloud** | New York fashion-rap fusion that merged cloud rap atmospherics with chopped-and-screwed influence, high-end visual identity, and crossover appeal. Polished production retaining the genre's spacious feel. | 125-145 | A$AP Rocky, A$AP Ferg, SpaceGhostPurrp |
| **Emo Cloud / SeshBoys** | Darker, more emotionally raw variant blending cloud rap production with emo and gothic lyrical themes. Lo-fi mixing, melancholic vocal delivery, and horror-influenced imagery. | 120-140 | Bones, Xavier Wulf, Chris Travis, Night Lovell |
| **Wave** | Mid-2010s evolution blending cloud rap atmospherics with contemporary EDM elements. Heavier synth work, more structured arrangements, and cleaner production while retaining the dreamy, reverb-soaked feel. | 130-150 | Kareem Lotfy, Sinjin Hawke, Klimeks |
| **Plugg** | Southern-derived substyle using cloud rap's atmospheric synths over tighter, more rhythmically precise trap patterns. Lighter, more melodic production with video game-influenced sound design. | 130-150 | Playboi Carti (early work), Thouxanbanfauni, UnoTheActivist |
| **GothBoiClique / Emo Rap** | Cloud rap's production merged with emo and pop-punk vocal melodies and confessional lyrics. Guitar samples layered under trap drums and ambient pads. Direct precursor to Lil Peep's mainstream breakthrough. | 120-140 | Lil Tracy, Wicca Phase Springs Eternal, Cold Hart |
| **Witch House Cloud** | Occult-tinged variant drawing from witch house's dark, distorted electronics. Heavily pitch-shifted vocals, horror atmospherics, and industrial textures layered over slow trap rhythms. | 110-135 | Black Kray, Goth Money Records |
| **Rare Phonk** | Late-2010s revival blending cloud rap's atmospheric approach with Memphis phonk's sample-based production. Chopped vocal loops, cowbell patterns, and lo-fi processing with cloud rap's spacious reverb. | 120-140 | DJ Smokey (crossover work), DJ Kenn AON |

## Reference Tracks

| Track | Artist | Context |
|-------|--------|---------|
| "I'm God" | Clams Casino (ft. Lil B) | Cloud rap's foundational track (2009). Built on a stretched, ethereal sample of Imogen Heap's "Just for Now," the instrumental defined the genre's sonic template: vast reverb, heavenly vocal chops, and trap percussion buried under ambient layers. Originally appeared on Lil B's *6 Kiss* before the instrumental version became a cult classic in its own right. |
| "Ginseng Strip 2002" | Yung Lean | 2013 viral breakthrough that established Stockholm's Sad Boys as a global force. Written when Lean was 16, the track pairs deadpan, brand-name-laden lyrics with Yung Sherman's icy synth production. The accompanying video's lo-fi internet aesthetics became inseparable from cloud rap's visual identity. |
| "Peso" | A$AP Rocky | 2011 debut single that brought cloud rap to mainstream hip-hop. Clams Casino's shimmering production under Rocky's effortless New York flow demonstrated the genre's crossover potential. Alongside "Purple Swag," it launched the A$AP Mob and proved cloud rap could coexist with commercial ambition. |
| "Numb / Getting Colder" | Flume ft. Kucka | While primarily electronic, this track captures the emotional and sonic palette cloud rap producers draw from: washed-out vocals, glacial textures, and a half-time pulse that prioritizes space over density. A useful reference for the genre's ambient production values. |
| "Hurt" | Yung Lean | From *Unknown Death 2002* (2013). Captures the Sad Boys sound at its most distilled: melancholic Auto-Tuned vocals, sparse icy synths, slow trap drums, and lyrics mixing consumer brands with emotional desolation. The track's cold beauty exemplifies why "sadness" became the genre's defining mood. |
| "Obedient" | Bladee & Ecco2K | Drain Gang at their most refined. Whitearmor's crystalline production layers glassy synths over minimal percussion while Bladee and Ecco2K trade ghostly, Auto-Tuned vocal lines. Demonstrates the Drain aesthetic: cold, precise, and hypnotic. |
| "Bass" | A$AP Rocky | From *Live.Love.A$AP* (2011). Clams Casino's production is pure cloud rap: pitch-shifted vocal samples float over booming 808s and cavernous reverb. Rocky's delivery matches the production's spaciousness, riding the beat rather than attacking it. |
| "Myself" | Bones | From *Garbage* (2014). Dark, lo-fi cloud rap with emo undertones. Sparse production, whispered vocals, and a creeping sense of isolation. Represents the Seshollowaterboyz branch of cloud rap: rawer, darker, and more emotionally exposed than the Sad Boys' ironic detachment. |
| "Still in Search of Sunshine" | Main Attrakionz | Early Bay Area cloud rap from the artists who inspired the genre's name. Hazy, sample-driven production with laid-back flows. Lo-fi and rough around the edges, capturing the genre before it coalesced into distinct subgenres. |
| "Underwater Tank" | Lil B | Based God over atmospheric production. Demonstrates Lil B's range beyond the "cooking" persona: contemplative, melodic, and riding ethereal beats that prioritize mood over impact. The spacious mixing and ambient textures are quintessential cloud rap. |
| "Dark & Handsome" | Night Lovell | Deep, rumbling bass paired with Night Lovell's signature baritone over minimal, dark cloud rap production. Represents the genre's heavier edge without losing the atmospheric spaciousness that defines the sound. |
| "Wrist Cry" | Bladee | From *Eversince* (2016). Whitearmor's production strips cloud rap to its barest elements: a single repeating synth phrase, ghostly ambient pads, and skeletal percussion. Bladee's heavily processed vocals blend into the mix like another instrument. Exemplifies the Drain Gang philosophy of reduction. |
