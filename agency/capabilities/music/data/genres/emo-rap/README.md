# Emo Rap

## Genre Overview

Emo rap is a fusion subgenre that merges hip-hop's trap production and vocal delivery with the emotional vulnerability, guitar-driven melodies, and confessional songwriting of emo and pop-punk. The genre's roots trace to the early-to-mid 2010s SoundCloud scene, where artists like Bones, Black Kray (Goth Money Records), and the GothBoiClique collective — including Wicca Phase Springs Eternal (formerly of the emo band Tigers Jaw), Lil Tracy, and Lil Peep — began layering anguished vocals over lo-fi trap beats with distorted guitar samples. Lil Peep's early mixtapes, particularly *Crybaby* (2016) and *Come Over When You're Sober, Pt. 1* (2017), crystallized the sound: 808 drums paired with emo guitar riffs, auto-tuned singing about heartbreak and substance abuse, and a visual aesthetic drawn directly from mid-2000s emo culture.

The genre exploded into the mainstream between 2017 and 2019, driven by three artists whose impact was amplified by tragedy. XXXTentacion's *17* (August 2017) stripped production to acoustic guitars and whispered vocals, reaching number two on the Billboard 200. His follow-up *?* (2018) debuted at number one before his murder in June 2018. Juice WRLD's *Goodbye & Good Riddance* (2018) brought melodic emo rap to pop radio, with "Lucid Dreams" peaking at number two on the Hot 100 by interpolating Fall Out Boy's "Sugar, We're Goin Down" producer. Lil Peep's death from a fentanyl overdose in November 2017 preceded the genre's commercial peak but cemented his status as its founding icon. By 2018, Spotify reported emo rap as its fastest-growing genre. Artists like Trippie Redd, iann dior, and nothing,nowhere continued expanding the sound's reach.

Today emo rap has matured beyond its SoundCloud origins into a durable cross-genre force. Artists like $uicideboy$, Yung Lean, and the late Mac Miller (whose *Swimming* explored adjacent emotional territory) demonstrated the style's range. The genre's influence extends into pop-punk revival (Machine Gun Kelly's *Tickets to My Downfall*), hyperpop, and mainstream trap. Contemporary acts like KennyHoopla, glaive, and Destroy Lonely carry forward its emotional directness while pushing production into new territory. The genre remains defined by its willingness to center mental health, substance use, heartbreak, and existential anxiety as primary lyrical concerns — a confessional honesty that resonates with listeners who grew up on both hip-hop and alternative rock.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Distorted electric guitars (often sampled or looped), 808 bass drums, trap hi-hats, acoustic guitar, piano and synth pads, reverb-heavy atmospheric layers; production blends trap drum programming with emo/pop-punk guitar tones; occasional live drums
- **Vocals**: Sung-rapped hybrid delivery with heavy auto-tune; melodic singing dominates over pure rapping; whispery and vulnerable tones common; screamed or shouted passages for emotional peaks; layered vocal harmonies and ad-libs; influence from pop-punk vocal phrasing
- **Production**: Lo-fi SoundCloud aesthetic in early works evolving to polished melodic trap; guitar loops (clean and distorted) over 808 patterns; spacious reverb and delay; melancholic minor-key melodies; atmospheric pads creating emotional weight; beat switches for dramatic effect
- **Energy/Mood**: Melancholic, vulnerable, anxious, heartbroken, nihilistic, nostalgic; oscillates between quiet despair and cathartic emotional outbursts; romanticization of pain and substance use; moments of defiant anger amid pervasive sadness
- **Structure**: Verse-chorus with emphasis on memorable melodic hooks; shorter songs (2-3 minutes common); bridges often feature emotional climax or tonal shift; intros and outros may use ambient textures or spoken word; beat switches within tracks
- **Tempo**: Typically 130-160 BPM with half-time feel (effective 65-80 BPM); slower ballad-style tracks at 70-85 BPM actual tempo; uptempo pop-punk influenced tracks reach 150-170 BPM with straight-time feel

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: AABB (couplet) or ABAB — simpler schemes than technical hip-hop; emphasis on emotional delivery over complex rhyme patterns; hook-driven structures where choruses carry the song
- **Rhyme quality**: Simple end rhymes and near rhymes acceptable; emotional authenticity valued over technical complexity; internal rhymes present but not mandatory; sung melodies often carry the rhyme naturally
- **Verse structure**: 8-12 bars per verse; shorter verses common as hooks dominate; pre-chorus sections bridge verse energy into chorus; spoken-word or whispered interludes between sections
- **Key rule**: Emotional honesty is non-negotiable — lyrics must sound confessional and personal, like diary entries set to music; specificity of pain (names, places, substances) creates authenticity
- **Avoid**: Braggadocio or tough-guy posturing that contradicts vulnerability; overly abstract imagery that distances from raw emotion; clinical or detached language about mental health (it should feel lived, not observed)
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **6 lines/verse** at half-time feel (130-150 BPM). Melodic singing needs space — do not exceed 8 lines. Acoustic/ballad tracks (70-85 BPM): max 4-6 lines. Topics: 1-2/verse.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **SoundCloud Emo Rap** | The original wave: lo-fi trap production with emo guitar samples, auto-tuned vocals, DIY aesthetic; raw and unpolished, distributed primarily through SoundCloud; defined the genre's visual and sonic identity | Lil Peep, Lil Tracy, GothBoiClique, Brennan Savage |
| **Melodic Emo Trap** | More polished production bridging emo rap with mainstream melodic trap; stronger hooks, cleaner mixes, radio-viable structures; retains emotional vulnerability but with commercial appeal | Juice WRLD, Trippie Redd, iann dior, The Kid LAROI |
| **Lo-Fi Emo Rap** | Stripped-down production emphasizing acoustic guitars, whispered vocals, and minimal beats; intimate and quiet; influenced by bedroom pop and indie folk as much as hip-hop | XXXTentacion (*17*), nothing,nowhere, guardin |
| **GothBoiClique / Goth Rap** | Darker variant merging emo rap with goth rock and post-punk aesthetics; heavier atmospheric production, occult visual imagery, bleaker lyrical outlook; rooted in the original GBC collective | Wicca Phase Springs Eternal, Cold Hart, Mackned, Horse Head |
| **Pop-Punk Rap** | Explicit fusion of pop-punk song structures, power chords, and vocal melodies with hip-hop delivery and trap production; the most accessible and commercially successful variant | Machine Gun Kelly, YUNGBLUD, KennyHoopla, nothing,nowhere |
| **Rage / Scream Emo** | High-energy variant featuring screamed vocals, distorted production, and aggressive delivery while maintaining emo's emotional confessional content; bridges emo rap with trap metal | XXXTentacion (aggressive tracks), Ski Mask the Slump God, Zillakami |
| **Cloud Emo** | Ethereal, reverb-drenched production with dreamy synths and subdued vocals; merges emo rap's emotional content with cloud rap's atmospheric production; introspective and hazy | Yung Lean, Bladee, Ecco2K, Crystal Castles influence |

## Reference Tracks

- **Lil Peep - "Awful Things" (feat. Lil Tracy)** — Emo rap's defining anthem: clean electric guitar loop over punchy 808s, Lil Peep's auto-tuned croon about toxic love and self-destruction, Lil Tracy's complementary verse; the template for every emo trap song that followed

- **Lil Peep - "Star Shopping"** — Acoustic guitar and minimal production stripped to essentials; Lil Peep's most intimate recording, singing about unrequited love with disarming simplicity; demonstrates emo rap's power at its most raw and unadorned

- **XXXTentacion - "Jocelyn Flores"** — *17*'s centerpiece: Potsu's sampled guitar loop, whispered vocals, and deeply personal grief narrative; proved emo rap could work with barely-there production and near-silence as an instrument

- **XXXTentacion - "SAD!"** — Number one posthumous hit combining emo melody with trap energy; the hook's simplicity and emotional directness connected with millions; showed emo rap's commercial ceiling was far higher than expected

- **Juice WRLD - "Lucid Dreams"** — Sting interpolation transformed into emo trap mega-hit; Juice WRLD's freestyle-derived melody and heartbreak lyrics over Nick Mira production; the song that brought emo rap to pop radio permanently

- **Juice WRLD - "All Girls Are the Same"** — Breakthrough single establishing Juice WRLD's formula: auto-tuned melodic singing, relationship pain over minor-key trap production; raw emotional honesty delivered with pop-song catchiness

- **nothing,nowhere - "Hammer"** — Genre-fluid emo rap at its most dynamic: shifts between whispered rap and screamed punk vocals within a single track; demonstrates the genre's range from quiet vulnerability to cathartic aggression

- **Wicca Phase Springs Eternal - "Just One More"** — GothBoiClique's darker edge: post-punk guitar textures, atmospheric synths, and deadpan delivery creating a nocturnal, haunted mood; the goth side of emo rap's DNA

- **$uicideboy$ - "...And to Those I Love, Thanks for Sticking Around"** — Emo rap's heaviest emotional payload: dual vocals trading verses about suicidal ideation and survival over melancholic production; devastating specificity and hard-earned hope

- **Trippie Redd - "Love Scars"** — Melodic emo trap with rock-adjacent vocal runs; Trippie's distinctive voice oscillating between singing and screaming; emotional volatility as musical structure

- **Bones - "CtrlAltDelete"** — Early SoundCloud emo rap prototype: lo-fi beats, whispered vocals, dark atmospheric production; Bones' prolific output and DIY aesthetic helped build the infrastructure the genre would later occupy

- **Lil Tracy - "Like a Farmer"** — GothBoiClique's melodic sensibility: catchy auto-tuned hook, emo guitar sample, playful-yet-sad energy; demonstrates the lighter, more accessible side of the original emo rap circle
