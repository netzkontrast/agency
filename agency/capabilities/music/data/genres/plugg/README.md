# Plugg

## Genre Overview

Plugg is a hip-hop production subgenre that emerged around 2013 from the Atlanta-based Beatpluggz collective, founded by producers MexikoDro and StoopidXool. The name derives from the "Plug!" producer tag the collective used on SoundCloud. The style drew from Zaytoven's gospel and soul-influenced trap production, Project Pat and Juicy J's Memphis rap, the snap rap of D4L, and — in a distinctive twist — the whimsical soundscapes of the Paper Mario Nintendo soundtrack. MexikoDro's production template established plugg's core identity: simple, hypnotic chord progressions, hard-hitting 808 bass, repetitive yet addictive melodies, and a spacey, almost dreamlike quality that distinguished it from the harder-edged trap production dominating Atlanta at the time. Early adopters included Playboi Carti, Diego Money, and Nebu Kiniza, who rapped and sang over these mesmerizing, loop-driven beats.

Plugg's evolution accelerated through its relationship with SoundCloud's DIY distribution ecosystem and its adoption by artists who would become mainstream stars. Playboi Carti's early work with MexikoDro helped define the vocal approach — minimalist, ad-lib-heavy, more about vibe than lyrical density. When Carti moved on to work with Pi'erre Bourne, the sonic DNA of plugg mutated into something adjacent but distinct: Pi'erre's spacey, melodic production on tracks like "Magnolia" (2017) and the *Die Lit* album (2018) carried plugg's dreamy quality into mainstream trap. Drake incorporated the style when MexikoDro produced "From Florida with Love" on *Dark Lane Demo Tapes* (2020). Meanwhile, a new variant called pluggnb (plugg and R&B) emerged in the late 2010s through producers like XanGang and Goyxrd, adding more melodic, intricate leads and denser drum programming influenced by contemporary R&B.

Today plugg has fractured into a constellation of substyles while maintaining its core identity as hip-hop's most melodically hypnotic production school. BoofPaxkMooky, Tony Shhnow, and 10kdunkin have carried the original MexikoDro-lineage plugg sound forward, while the pluggnb variant — popularized by Lil Tecca, Autumn!, SSGKobe, and SoFaygo — has become a dominant force on TikTok and streaming playlists. Cash Cobain's collaboration with StoopidXool and FLEE produced the viral pluggnb hit "SWISH / USE 2" featuring Brent Faiyaz (2020). The genre's influence extends beyond its practitioners: plugg's emphasis on melody, space, and hypnotic repetition has shaped mainstream trap production, and its DIY SoundCloud infrastructure pioneered distribution patterns that other underground scenes would adopt.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Synthesized melodies (bells, chimes, plucks, flutes), 808 bass patterns, crisp snares and claps, rapid hi-hats, spacey synth pads, video-game-inspired sound design, Rhodes/electric piano textures; minimal instrumentation with maximum melodic impact; no live instruments in traditional plugg
- **Vocals**: Minimalist delivery; ad-lib-heavy with sparse lyrical content; mumble-influenced vocal style; auto-tune common but lighter than melodic trap; vocals function as another textural element rather than lyrical centerpiece; sung-rapped hybrid in pluggnb variant; vocal chains and harmonies in R&B-influenced variants
- **Production**: Hypnotic looping melodies, spacey atmospheres, clean mix with prominent bass, simple chord progressions that create trance-like repetition; 808 bass patterns are central and melodic; hi-hat patterns range from simple to intricate rolls; deliberate use of space and silence; video-game and Nintendo-influenced sound selection
- **Energy/Mood**: Dreamy, hypnotic, spacey, laid-back yet rhythmically engaging, euphoric, cool; a floating quality that distinguishes it from trap's aggression; slightly nostalgic or wistful; pluggnb adds romantic and sensual undertones; night-driving energy without phonk's darkness
- **Structure**: Simple verse-hook structures; hooks built on melodic vocal loops; short songs (2-3 minutes typical); minimal arrangement with few sections; repetition is a feature not a bug — the hypnotic loop IS the song; beat drops and 808 slides create dynamic variation within minimalist structures
- **Tempo**: Typically 130-160 BPM with half-time feel (effective 65-80 BPM); pluggnb sits slightly slower at 120-140 BPM for smoother R&B feel; uptempo plugg can reach 170 BPM; the half-time 808 pattern is essential to the groove

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: AABB (couplet) — simple and direct; rhyme complexity is secondary to melodic flow and vibe; near rhymes and simple end rhymes standard
- **Rhyme quality**: Simple end rhymes acceptable; slang-heavy and conversational; the vibe and cadence matter more than rhyme density; hooks rely on melodic catchiness rather than lyrical cleverness
- **Verse structure**: 8-12 bars; shorter verses common; ad-libs and vocal textures fill space between lines; pre-hooks and post-hooks built from repeated phrases; some tracks are essentially all hook with minimal verse content
- **Key rule**: Less is more — the beat is the star; vocals should complement the hypnotic melody, not compete with it; one clear vibe per track, no sudden tonal shifts
- **Avoid**: Dense wordplay that fights the dreamy production; aggressive delivery that breaks the floating feel; too many ideas per verse (plugg rewards simplicity and repetition); overlong verses that disrupt the loop's trance
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **4-6 lines/verse** at half-time feel (130-160 BPM). Plugg is spacious — Suno needs room for the beat to breathe. Pluggnb: 6 lines max with melodic singing. Topics: 1/verse max, keep it vibes-focused.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Classic Plugg** | The original Beatpluggz sound: simple chord progressions, hard 808s, hypnotic melodies, spacey atmosphere; lo-fi SoundCloud origins; the purest expression of the style's minimalist, trance-like quality | MexikoDro, StoopidXool, PoloBoyShawty, A$att |
| **Pluggnb** | Plugg merged with contemporary R&B; more melodic and intricate leads, denser drum programming, sung vocals, romantic and sensual themes; the genre's most commercially successful evolution | XanGang, Goyxrd, Cash Cobain, StoopidXool |
| **Spacey Plugg** | Emphasizes the atmospheric, floaty quality; reverb-drenched synths, ambient pads, minimal drums, dreamlike soundscapes; the most psychedelic plugg variant; night-driving music | Pi'erre Bourne (adjacent), Yung Bans productions, Ethereal |
| **Hard Plugg** | Heavier 808 patterns, more aggressive hi-hat rolls, slightly darker melodic choices; bridges plugg's melodic approach with traditional trap energy; louder and more commanding | Tony Shhnow, 10kdunkin, Lucki (as rapper over plugg beats) |
| **Nintendo Plugg** | Leans fully into the video-game sound palette: chiptune melodies, 8-bit textures, Mario and Zelda-inspired sound design over plugg drum patterns; playful and nostalgic | MexikoDro (early work), various SoundCloud producers |
| **Plugg Rap** | Emphasizes rapping over plugg beats rather than the sung/mumbled approach; more lyrical content while maintaining the production aesthetic; the most hip-hop-traditional variant | Playboi Carti (early), Diego Money, BoofPaxkMooky, FLEE |
| **Pop Plugg** | Accessible, hook-driven variant designed for streaming playlists and TikTok virality; cleaner production, poppier melodies, broader appeal; the commercial mainstream of the plugg ecosystem | Lil Tecca, SoFaygo, SSGKobe, Autumn! |

## Reference Tracks

- **MexikoDro - "Round 1"** — Plugg production's origin point: the hypnotic bell melody, punchy 808s, and spacey atmosphere that defined the Beatpluggz sound; minimalist yet addictive, demonstrating how a simple loop can create its own gravity

- **Playboi Carti - "Broke Boi"** — Early Carti over classic plugg production: minimal lyrics, maximum vibe; the vocal approach — sparse, ad-lib-heavy, more texture than content — that would define how artists rap over plugg beats

- **Playboi Carti - "Magnolia" (prod. Pi'erre Bourne)** — Plugg DNA mutating into mainstream trap: Pi'erre Bourne's spacey flute melody, Carti's iconic ad-libs, the "in New York I Milly Rock" hook; proved plugg's melodic sensibility could reach the Billboard Hot 100

- **Playboi Carti - "Shoota" (feat. Lil Uzi Vert)** — Plugg-adjacent chemistry at its peak: Pi'erre Bourne production, two complementary vocal styles trading energy over a dreamy beat; demonstrated how plugg's spacey production could support dynamic performances

- **Cash Cobain & StoopidXool - "SWISH / USE 2" (feat. Brent Faiyaz & FLEE)** — Pluggnb's defining moment: StoopidXool's production bridging classic plugg with R&B smoothness, Brent Faiyaz's vocals adding soul, FLEE's rap grounding it in hip-hop; the track that proved pluggnb was a viable commercial style

- **Lucki - "Greed"** — Rapper-over-plugg perfected: hazy delivery floating over spacey production; Lucki's detached cool complementing the beat's hypnotic quality; demonstrates plugg's capacity to support genuine lyrical content without losing its dreamy character

- **Tony Shhnow - "Plugged In"** — Hard plugg: aggressive 808 patterns meeting melodic plugg melodies; proves the style can hit hard without abandoning its essential character; the next generation carrying the sound forward

- **Lil Tecca - "Show Me Up"** — Pluggnb meets TikTok virality: catchy melodic hook, clean production, accessible energy; demonstrates how plugg's melodic DNA travels into pop-facing contexts while retaining its hypnotic core

- **SoFaygo - "Thrax"** — Pluggnb energy channeled into high-octane delivery: melodic yet intense, hooky yet underground-credible; another TikTok breakthrough that widened plugg's audience without diluting its sound

- **Diego Money - "Choppa"** — Early plugg vocal style: simple cadence, repeated phrases, the beat doing the heavy lifting; alongside Carti, helped establish the minimalist vocal approach that became plugg's default

- **MexikoDro - "2017"** — Late-era classic plugg: refined but true to the original vision; demonstrates how the sound matured while staying committed to its core principles of melody, space, and hypnotic repetition
