# Cyberpunk

## Genre Overview

Cyberpunk music is an electronic genre rooted in the dystopian science fiction aesthetic of the 1980s, drawing from the neon-lit, tech-noir worlds of William Gibson's *Neuromancer* (1984) and Ridley Scott's *Blade Runner* (1982). The sonic foundation was laid by film composers Vangelis, John Carpenter, and Tangerine Dream, whose analog synthesizer scores established the template of dark, atmospheric electronic music evoking urban decay and technological overreach. Italian producer Giorgio Moroder's sequencer-driven production on Donna Summer's "I Feel Love" (1977) and his *Blade Runner*-era work further cemented the connection between electronic music and futuristic imagery. By the mid-2000s, these influences coalesced into a distinct production style as French house artists like Kavinsky and College began reviving 1980s synthesizer aesthetics, setting the stage for what would become synthwave and its darker offshoots.

The genre crystallized in the early 2010s when a wave of producers, many from metal and industrial backgrounds, pushed the synthwave template into aggressive, horror-inflected territory. Perturbator's *Dangerous Days* (2014) and Carpenter Brut's *Trilogy* (2015) defined the darksynth variant, replacing nostalgic warmth with distorted basslines, screaming leads, and cinematic intensity. GosT brought black metal aesthetics into the mix with *Behemoth* (2015), while Daniel Deluxe contributed a cleaner, more cinematic approach. The 2011 film *Drive*, featuring Kavinsky and College on its soundtrack, became a cultural watershed that brought retrowave aesthetics to mainstream audiences and created a pipeline of new listeners into the darker corners of the scene.

The release of CD Projekt Red's *Cyberpunk 2077* in 2020 pushed cyberpunk music further into mainstream consciousness, with its soundtrack blending industrial, hip-hop, and electronic styles under a unified aesthetic. Today, cyberpunk music exists as a broad umbrella encompassing everything from the aggressive darksynth of Perturbator to the cinematic ambient of Makeup and Vanity Set. The genre maintains strong ties to gaming culture, film scoring, and visual art, functioning as much as an aesthetic movement as a purely musical one. Labels like Blood Music, NewRetroWave, and Lazerdiscs Records continue to serve as curatorial hubs for the scene.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Analog and virtual synthesizers (Moog, Roland Juno, Prophet), arpeggiated basslines, distorted leads, orchestral stabs, drum machines (Roland TR-808, TR-909), electric guitar (in darksynth variants), vocoder and talk box, cinematic string pads
- **Vocals**: Often instrumental; when present, typically processed through vocoders or heavy effects, spoken-word narration, pitched and distorted vocals, occasionally clean singing in more melodic variants; samples from science fiction films common
- **Production**: Heavy sidechain compression, layered synthesizer stacks, aggressive distortion on bass and leads, cinematic reverb, retro-futuristic mixing aesthetic that emulates 1980s film scores while using modern production techniques, tape saturation and analog warmth
- **Energy/Mood**: Dark, intense, dystopian, nocturnal, cinematic, aggressive in darksynth variants, melancholic and atmospheric in ambient variants; emotional range spans from adrenaline-fueled action to contemplative urban isolation
- **Structure**: Intro-build-drop format common in darksynth; more progressive, soundtrack-style structures in cinematic variants; typically 3-6 minutes; instrumental tracks often feature extended atmospheric passages
- **Tempo**: 100-140 BPM typically; darksynth tends toward 110-130 BPM with driving rhythm; ambient/cinematic variants slower at 80-100 BPM; outrun variants faster at 130-150 BPM

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: Flexible; ABAB or AABB when structured. Many tracks are instrumental.
- **Rhyme quality**: Clean but not flashy. Imagery over wordplay.
- **Verse structure**: Short verses, 4-6 lines. Spoken word or narrative passages common.
- **Key rule**: Atmosphere and world-building drive everything. Lyrics serve the cinematic vision, not the other way around.
- **Avoid**: Bright or optimistic imagery that contradicts the dystopian aesthetic. Overly literal sci-fi references that feel like fan fiction.
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **4 lines/verse** at 110-130 BPM. Often instrumental. When lyrics present, sparse and atmospheric. Topics: 1/verse.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Darksynth** | Aggressive, distorted synthwave with metal and industrial influences; heavy basslines, screaming leads, horror-film intensity; the most energetic cyberpunk variant | Perturbator, Carpenter Brut, GosT, Dan Terminus |
| **Outrun** | High-energy, driving synthwave evoking nighttime highway chases; arpeggiated basslines, pulsing drums, neon-lit optimism balanced with dark undertones | Kavinsky, Lazerhawk, Miami Nights 1984, Power Glove |
| **Cinematic Cyberpunk** | Film-score-influenced compositions with orchestral elements, ambient passages, and narrative arc; designed for storytelling and visual media | Makeup and Vanity Set, S U R V I V E, Daniel Deluxe |
| **Industrial Cyberpunk** | Crossover with EBM and industrial; harsher textures, mechanical rhythms, factory-floor aesthetic merged with synthwave melodics | 3TEETH, Author & Punisher, Street Cleaner |
| **Cyberpunk Ambient** | Atmospheric, slow-burning soundscapes evoking rain-slicked cityscapes and neon reflections; minimal rhythmic content, heavy on texture and mood | Makeup and Vanity Set, Perturbator (ambient works), Cliff Martinez |
| **Synthwave (Classic)** | The broader parent genre; warmer, more nostalgic 1980s revival with less overt darkness; melodic synths, clean production, retro drums | Com Truise, FM-84, The Midnight, Timecop1983 |
| **Cybersynth** | Merges cyberpunk aesthetics with modern bass music and EDM production; heavier drops, festival-oriented energy, contemporary sound design | Dance With the Dead, Mega Drive, Volkor X |

## Reference Tracks

- **Vangelis - "Blade Runner Blues"** — The foundational cyberpunk soundtrack: melancholic analog synthesizers over rain-drenched cityscapes, establishing the emotional vocabulary that every cyberpunk producer since has drawn from; proved electronic music could convey deep narrative emotion

- **John Carpenter - "Escape from New York (Main Theme)"** — Minimalist synth scoring that demonstrated how a single synthesizer line could create entire worlds of tension and atmosphere; Carpenter's DIY approach to film scoring became a direct template for darksynth producers

- **Kavinsky - "Nightcall"** — The outrun anthem that soundtracked *Drive* (2011) and introduced millions to retrowave aesthetics; vocoder vocals over pulsing arpeggios created the definitive template for nocturnal synthwave

- **Perturbator - "Future Club"** — Darksynth crystallized: aggressive distorted basslines, horror-film tension, and relentless drive; from *Dangerous Days* (2014), this track defined the aggressive wing of cyberpunk music

- **Carpenter Brut - "Turbo Killer"** — Theatrical darksynth at its peak: metal-influenced intensity, operatic builds, and cinematic scope that proved synthwave could rival metal for sheer energy; the accompanying music video became iconic

- **GosT - "Behemoth"** — Black metal aesthetics applied to synthwave: blast-beat energy, occult atmosphere, extreme distortion; pushed the genre's boundaries further than anyone expected

- **Daniel Deluxe - "Ghosts"** — Clean, cinematic darksynth designed for the *Ghostrunner* game soundtrack; demonstrates how cyberpunk music functions as interactive media scoring

- **Perturbator - "She Is Young, She Is Beautiful, She Is Next"** — Atmospheric cyberpunk that balances aggression with vulnerability; vocal samples and slow-building tension showcase the genre's emotional range beyond pure intensity

- **Mega Drive - "Acid Spit"** — Raw, gritty cybersynth with industrial textures; evokes the grimy underside of cyberpunk cityscapes rather than their neon glamour

- **Com Truise - "Brokendate"** — Melodic synthwave warmth: lush analog pads, mid-tempo groove, and nostalgic futurism; represents the gentler side of the cyberpunk spectrum

- **S U R V I V E - "Dirge"** — Modular synthesizer exploration that bridges cyberpunk and experimental electronic music; the sonic world-building that led to their *Stranger Things* scoring work

- **Dance With the Dead - "Robeast"** — Guitar-driven darksynth: metal riffs layered over synthwave production, bridging the rock and electronic worlds with aggressive energy and melodic hooks
