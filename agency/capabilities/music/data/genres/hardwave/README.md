# Hardwave

## Genre Overview

Hardwave crystallized around 2017-2019 as a distinct intensification of the wave genre, pushing its atmospheric foundations toward greater aggression, heavier bass, and harder-hitting percussion while retaining the emotional depth and reverb-drenched aesthetics that defined wave's core identity. Where classic wave pursued melancholy through restraint, hardwave pursued it through overwhelming force — the same loneliness and urban isolation expressed at higher volume and with greater physical impact. Key producers like Deadcrow, Juuku, and Ytho took the half-time trap rhythms, pitched vocal samples, and ethereal synth pads of wave and fortified them with grime-influenced bass weight, distorted 808s, and percussion that hit with genuine physical force.

The genre emerged from the same SoundCloud ecosystem that birthed wave, with producers pushing toward heavier territory in response to both the grime revival and the broader bass music scene. The Aerobics label and collective became a significant hub for the sound, releasing music that occupied the space between wave's atmospheric beauty and grime's rhythmic aggression. Hardwave's rhythmic foundation remains half-time — the kicks and snares land at the halved tempo feel while hi-hats and auxiliary percussion operate at full speed — but the bass hits harder, the synths carry more distortion, and the overall energy is closer to a bass music set than an ambient listening session. The genre retained wave's emotional core while making it viable for heavier DJ sets.

Hardwave continues to evolve as part of the broader wave ecosystem, with producers moving fluidly between atmospheric and aggressive modes. The genre's influence is visible in adjacent scenes — colour bass, melodic dubstep, and future bass all show hardwave's fingerprints in their combination of emotional melody with bass weight. Newer artists continue to push the boundaries, incorporating elements of drum and bass, phonk, and industrial into the hardwave template while maintaining the atmospheric identity that distinguishes it from straightforward bass music. The genre represents wave's proof of concept: that atmospheric, emotional electronic music can coexist with genuine dancefloor (and moshpit) intensity.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Heavy 808 sub-bass and kicks (often distorted or saturated), grime-influenced bass stabs, detuned synth pads with reverb, aggressive lead synths, half-time trap percussion (punchy snares, rapid hi-hats), pitched vocal samples, atmospheric textures; the balance shifts from wave's synth-pad dominance toward bass and percussion prominence while maintaining atmospheric layering

- **Vocals**: Pitched and heavily processed vocal samples (inherited from wave); more aggressive sampling choices — sometimes rap or grime vocals rather than R&B; vocal chops used rhythmically; occasional original vocals with heavy processing; distortion and bitcrushing applied more liberally than in classic wave; the spectral quality remains but with added grit

- **Production**: Heavier compression and limiting than classic wave; distorted or saturated 808s; more prominent drum bus processing; grime-influenced bass design (wobbles, stabs, reese bass); reverb still prominent but competing with more upfront elements; sidechain compression more aggressive; stereo width manipulation; sub-bass tuned for club systems; mastering aimed at competitive loudness while preserving atmospheric depth

- **Energy/Mood**: Aggressive melancholy; emotional intensity expressed through force; dark, nocturnal, and introspective like wave but with physical impact; the feeling of driving too fast through empty streets at night; cathartic rather than contemplative; adrenaline-laced sadness; simultaneously beautiful and violent

- **Structure**: Build-drop structures more pronounced than in classic wave; atmospheric intros leading to heavy drops; breakdowns that strip back to reverb-drenched pads before the bass returns; half-time drops with full-time percussion; bridges that shift between atmospheric and aggressive modes; tracks 3-5 minutes with clear dynamic arc

- **Tempo**: 140-170 BPM in half-time (effectively 70-85 BPM feel); sits at the higher end of wave's tempo range; some tracks push into drum and bass territory (170+) while maintaining half-time feel

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: Free or ABCB — impressionistic like wave but with more urgency; short, impactful phrases
- **Rhyme quality**: Harder consonants and more percussive word choices than classic wave; near rhymes acceptable; words chosen for emotional and sonic impact
- **Verse structure**: 2-4 lines per verse; sparse but delivered with more force than classic wave; fragments that hit like the bass drops they accompany
- **Key rule**: Write with the same emotional vocabulary as wave — loneliness, urban isolation, dark beauty — but deliver it with intensity. Lyrics should feel like shouting into a void rather than whispering. Short, punchy phrases that survive heavy vocal processing. The aggression serves the emotion, not the other way around.
- **Avoid**: Extended narrative; gentle or delicate phrasing that cannot survive the heavy production; overly wordy passages; anything that requires quiet, nuanced delivery; positivity or lightness that contradicts the dark energy
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **2-3 lines/verse** at 140-170 BPM half-time. Punchy, emotionally charged phrasing. Leave space for bass drops and atmospheric breakdowns. Topics: 1/track. Aggressive melancholy. Intense delivery with heavy processing.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Core Hardwave** | The central template: wave's atmosphere with grime-influenced bass weight and harder drums; emotional but physically impactful | Deadcrow, Ytho |
| **Melodic Hardwave** | Retains more of wave's melodic elements — prominent synth melodies and emotional chord progressions — while adding bass weight | Juuku, Skeler (harder works) |
| **Grime-Wave** | Stronger grime influence in rhythmic patterns, bass design, and percussion; UK bass music energy within wave's atmospheric framework | Deadcrow, Plastician (wave sets) |
| **Industrial Hardwave** | Incorporating industrial noise textures, harsher distortion, and more mechanical rhythms into the hardwave template; the genre's darkest edge | Various producers at the genre boundary |
| **Phonk-Wave Crossover** | Memphis rap samples, cowbell patterns, and phonk's distorted aesthetic merged with hardwave's atmospheric bass heaviness | Various producers blending both scenes |

## Reference Tracks

- **Deadcrow - "Search"** — Hardwave's defining track: wave's atmospheric beauty colliding with grime-weight bass and hard-hitting percussion; proves that emotional depth and physical intensity are not mutually exclusive; the track that drew the line between wave and hardwave

- **Juuku - "missing u"** — Melodic hardwave at its most emotionally devastating: heavy 808s and atmospheric pads carrying genuine grief; demonstrates how hardwave's aggression can amplify rather than obscure emotional vulnerability; accessible without sacrificing weight

- **Ytho - "Nightfall"** — Dark, aggressive hardwave that pushes toward the genre's most intense territory; grime-influenced rhythms under reverb-drenched atmosphere; the nocturnal menace of wave expressed at maximum volume

- **Deadcrow - "Elevate"** — Grime-wave crossover that exemplifies the Aerobics collective's sound; UK bass energy channeled through wave's atmospheric lens; the drop hits with physical force while the breakdown floats in reverb

- **Skeler - "Arcadia"** — Harder Skeler production that bridges classic wave and hardwave; melodic enough for wave purists, heavy enough for bass music fans; demonstrates the spectrum between atmospheric and aggressive within the wave ecosystem

- **Barnacle Boi - "Drift"** — Atmospheric hardwave with patient builds and devastating drops; the tension between beauty and aggression sustained across the full track; represents the generation of producers who grew up within the wave scene and pushed it heavier
