## Reference Tracks

### FM-84
- "Running in the Night" (feat. Ollie Wride) (Atlas, 2016) — Synthwave's defining vocal track; sparkling arpeggios, soaring tenor, 80s AOR meets modern retrowave
- "Wild Ones" (feat. Ollie Wride) (Atlas, 2016) — Uptempo anthemic synthwave; exuberant chorus and driving production
- "Everything" (Atlas, 2016) — Instrumental opener; shimmering synths, cinematic atmosphere, golden-hour mood
- "Goodbye" (feat. Clive Farrington) (Atlas, 2016) — When in Rome vocalist; full-circle 80s synth-pop moment

### GUNSHIP
- "Tech Noir" (GUNSHIP, 2015) — John Carpenter narration, Charlie Simpson guest vocals; claymation video by Lee Hardcastle; cinematic synthwave landmark
- "Dark All Day" (feat. Tim Cappello & Indiana) (Dark All Day, 2018) — Tim Cappello's Lost Boys saxophone over driving synthwave; animated vampire-slayer video
- "Fly For Your Life" (GUNSHIP, 2015) — Pure synthwave momentum; powerful vocals over layered analog synths
- "When You Grow Up, Your Heart Dies" (Dark All Day, 2018) — Breakfast Club reference; fan-submitted recordings with Wil Wheaton; emotionally communal
- "Doom Dance" (feat. Carpenter Brut & Gavin Rossdale) (Unicorn, 2023) — Darksynth-rock collision; peak guest feature ambition
- "Tech Noir 2" (feat. John Carpenter & Charlie Simpson) (Tech Noir Timelines EP, 2025) — Sequel with returning collaborators; claymation by Lee Hardcastle
- "Black Blood Red Kiss" (feat. Kat Von D) (Dark All Day, 2018) — Gothic vocal; haunting and seductive darkwave
- "The Drone Racing League" (Dark All Day, 2018) — Cyberpunk instrumental energy; released as glow-in-the-dark 7" vinyl

### The Midnight
- "Sunset" (Endless Summer, 2016) — Signature track; warm synths, gentle sax, earnest vocals, melancholy meets optimism
- "Vampires" (Endless Summer, 2016) — Sax-heavy midnight drive anthem; dark, romantic, propulsive
- "Jason" (feat. Nikki Flores) (Endless Summer, 2016) — Saxophone-driven storytelling with guest vocals
- "Los Angeles" (Nocturnal, 2017) — Dreamy, nostalgic anthem; neon-noir longing
- "Monsters" (Monsters, 2020) — Billboard 200 debut track; emotionally direct, expansive production
- "Days of Thunder" (Days of Thunder EP, 2014) — The debut; saxophone-driven, warm, retro template
- "Gloria" (Days of Thunder EP, 2014) — One of the duo's first songs; pure chemistry
- "Dance With Somebody" (Monsters, 2020) — Full sonic palette: sax, synths, warm vocals, irresistible hook

### Timecop1983
- "On the Run" (Night Drive, 2018) — Bittersweet instrumental dreamwave; intimate and nostalgic
- "Journeys" (Journeys, 2014) — Early dreamwave; establishes melancholic signature sound
