# Chopped and Screwed

## Genre Overview

Chopped and screwed is a remix technique and genre that originated in Houston, Texas, in the early 1990s, invented by DJ Screw (Robert Earl Davis Jr.). The technique involves slowing down the tempo of a track to 60-70 BPM (from its original speed), then "chopping" — manipulating the record by skipping, stuttering, and repeating segments — to create a syrupy, druggy, hypnotic version of the source material. DJ Screw developed this approach through hundreds of mixtapes ("Screw tapes") that he sold from his house and local record shops, building a devoted following in Houston's South Side before the sound spread across the American South and eventually the world.

The genre is inseparable from Houston's car culture and the experience of cruising slowly through the city's sprawling streets. The slowed tempo was designed to complement this pace — music for sipping lean (codeine cough syrup mixed with soda), riding with the top down, and letting the bass rattle through a car's sound system. DJ Screw's technique transformed familiar hip-hop and R&B tracks into something entirely new: melodies became mournful, vocals dropped to an otherworldly pitch, and beats took on a heavy, narcotic gravity. The aesthetic was both simple and profound — slowing down revealed hidden textures and emotional depths in songs that were never apparent at normal speed.

DJ Screw's death in 2000 from a codeine overdose at age 29 cast a tragic shadow over the genre, but his legacy only grew. The Screwed Up Click — his crew of rappers and DJs including Big Moe, Fat Pat, Lil' Keke, and Z-Ro — continued the tradition. The chopped and screwed technique became standard practice in Southern hip-hop, with producers like Michael "5000" Watts and OG Ron C of Swishahouse creating official chopped and screwed versions of major label releases. The influence extends far beyond Houston: Drake, A$AP Rocky, and Travis Scott have all drawn heavily on the aesthetic, and the slowed-and-reverb trend on YouTube and TikTok is a direct descendant of DJ Screw's innovation.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Source material is typically hip-hop or R&B; the "instrumentation" is the remix technique itself — turntables, pitch control, and tape manipulation; when original chopped and screwed productions exist, they use deep 808 bass, slow drum machine patterns, syrupy synth pads, and minimal melodic elements; sub-bass is critical, designed for car stereo systems

- **Vocals**: Pitched down significantly from original recordings, creating a deep, distorted, otherworldly quality; vocals may be stuttered, repeated, or reversed through the chopping technique; the pitch-shifted vocals give a surreal, dreamlike character; when featuring original rapping, delivery is slow, deliberate, and bass-heavy

- **Production**: Slowed tempo (typically 60-70 BPM effective speed), chopping (skipping/repeating sections via turntable manipulation), heavy emphasis on sub-bass and low frequencies, reverb and delay added to enhance the syrupy atmosphere, minimal EQ — the lo-fi quality of tape dubbing was part of the original aesthetic; modern versions may be cleaner but maintain the slow, heavy character

- **Energy/Mood**: Hypnotic, narcotic, heavy, meditative; the pace of driving slowly through Houston at night; melancholic and introspective despite often featuring party-oriented source material; psychedelic in its distortion of familiar music; simultaneously relaxed and emotionally weighty; the mood of purple drank and slow cruising

- **Structure**: Follows the structure of the source material but with chopping disruptions — sections may repeat, skip, or stutter; intros are often extended; hooks may be chopped and repeated multiple times; the DJ's manipulation creates a new structural logic; original compositions follow verse-hook structures with very slow tempos and extended instrumental sections

- **Tempo**: 60-70 BPM (slowed from original tempos of 85-140 BPM)

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: AABB — couplet-based, matching the slow, deliberate delivery; simple schemes that maintain clarity at reduced speed
- **Rhyme quality**: Clean, hard-hitting end rhymes; multisyllabic rhymes work well at slow tempos because each syllable has space; words with bass-heavy vowel sounds ("low," "slow," "roll," "blow") complement the slowed aesthetic
- **Verse structure**: 8-16 lines per verse; slow delivery means fewer words per bar but each word carries more weight
- **Key rule**: Write for slow motion. Every word will be stretched and deepened — choose words that sound good pitched down. Imagery of cruising, nighttime, and Houston streets. The slow tempo gives each line enormous weight, so every bar must justify its space.
- **Avoid**: Rapid-fire delivery; high-energy party lyrics that clash with the slowed mood; complex tongue-twisting phrases that become unintelligible when pitched down; bright or upbeat imagery
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **6-8 lines/verse** at 60-70 BPM. Slow, deliberate delivery with space between lines. Heavy, bass-forward sound. Topics: 1/track. Cruising, nightlife, Houston pride, street life, introspection.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Classic Screw** | DJ Screw's original technique: slowed and chopped remixes of hip-hop and R&B on mixtapes; raw, lo-fi, tape-dubbed | DJ Screw |
| **Swishahouse Style** | More polished, commercially oriented chopped and screwed remixes; official versions of major label releases | Michael "5000" Watts, OG Ron C |
| **Original Screw Compositions** | Original music composed at chopped and screwed tempos rather than remixes; Houston artists recording specifically for the slow sound | Big Moe, Z-Ro, Lil' Keke |
| **Slowed + Reverb** | Internet-era descendant: tracks simply slowed with reverb added (no chopping); YouTube/TikTok phenomenon | Various anonymous uploaders |
| **Screwed R&B** | R&B tracks given the chop and screw treatment; the slowed vocals create an especially emotional, melancholic effect | DJ Screw (R&B selections), Various |

## Reference Tracks

- **DJ Screw - "June 27th" (Screw Tape)** — The most legendary Screw tape: a freestyle session featuring the entire Screwed Up Click, slowed to a syrupy crawl; captures the communal, late-night atmosphere that defined the movement and became a Houston cultural touchstone

- **Big Moe - "Purple Stuff"** — Original composition designed for the screwed aesthetic: Big Moe's melodic, Auto-Tune-pioneering vocal style over slow, syrupy production; the sonic equivalent of lean culture distilled into a single track

- **DJ Screw - "Wineberry Over Gold" (remix)** — DJ Screw's treatment of UGK's track demonstrates his technique at its most transformative: the slowed tempo reveals emotional depths invisible at normal speed, turning a hard rap track into something haunting

- **Lil' Keke - "Southside"** — A Houston anthem first heard on DJ Screw tapes before becoming an official release; the track that proved chopped and screwed could produce original hits, not just remixes

- **Z-Ro - "I Found Me"** — Exemplifies the emotional power of the screwed aesthetic: Z-Ro's already-vulnerable vocal delivery becomes devastating when slowed and deepened; street music with genuine pathos

- **Fat Pat - "Tops Drop"** — Classic Houston track that became a posthumous anthem; its laid-back groove and Fat Pat's smooth delivery are the platonic ideal of music made for slow cruising through Houston streets
