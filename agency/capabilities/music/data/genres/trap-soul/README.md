# Trap Soul

## Genre Overview

Trap soul is a fusion of contemporary R&B vocal stylings with trap music's 808-driven production, creating a sound that pairs emotional, melodic singing with the hard-hitting drums and dark atmospheres of hip-hop. The genre's foundations were laid by several converging currents in the early 2010s: Kanye West's *808s & Heartbreak* (2008) pioneered the concept of vulnerable auto-tuned singing over minimalist electronic production; The Weeknd's *House of Balloons* (2011) and PARTYNEXTDOOR's early work established dark, moody R&B over trap-adjacent beats in Toronto; and Future's melodic trap recordings demonstrated how auto-tuned vocals could carry emotional weight over 808 bass patterns. These influences collided when Louisville singer Bryson Tiller released *T R A P S O U L* on October 2, 2015 via RCA Records — the album that gave the genre its name and its commercial blueprint.

*T R A P S O U L* was certified 5x Platinum by the RIAA and established the core formula: intimate R&B vocal delivery — Tiller singing and rapping about relationships with conversational directness — over production that featured prominent 808 kick drums, hi-hat rolls, and synth pads typically associated with trap. The album's success spawned a wave of artists who refined the approach. 6LACK's *Free 6LACK* (November 2016) brought minimalist darkness and Atlanta cool. Tory Lanez's *I Told You* (August 2016) added aggressive confidence. SZA's *Ctrl* (June 2017), while broader than strict trap soul, incorporated the production aesthetic into a more expansive R&B vision. By 2019, Summer Walker's *Over It* — executive produced by London on da Track — became the highest-charting debut by a female R&B artist in over a decade, cementing trap soul as the dominant R&B production style of the late 2010s.

Trap soul has become the default production language of contemporary R&B, influencing artists far beyond its original practitioners. H.E.R., Kehlani, and dvsn added neo-soul and gospel inflections to the trap-R&B foundation. Brent Faiyaz emerged as the genre's coolest modern voice with *Wasteland* (2022). The style has also circled back to influence hip-hop, with rap artists like Drake, PartyNextDoor, and Ty Dolla $ign moving fluidly between trap soul and rap. While some critics argue the label has become so broad as to be meaningless — virtually all modern R&B uses some trap production elements — the genre retains a distinct identity through its specific emotional register: nocturnal, intimate, emotionally vulnerable but never soft, with a production aesthetic that keeps one foot in the street and one in the bedroom.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: 808 bass drums and sub-bass, trap hi-hats (open and closed rolls), crisp snares and claps, atmospheric synth pads, Rhodes and electric piano, minimal guitar (clean tones), vocal layering and harmonies, subtle strings, reverb-heavy ambient textures; production is spacious and dark with R&B warmth
- **Vocals**: Melodic R&B singing with contemporary hip-hop influence; auto-tune as stylistic choice (subtle to prominent); falsetto and head voice common; whispered and breathy tones for intimacy; occasional rapped verses or spoken passages; vocal runs and melisma from R&B tradition; layered harmonies and background vocals
- **Production**: Trap drum patterns (808 kicks, hi-hat rolls, snare patterns) under R&B harmonic frameworks; dark, atmospheric mixes with prominent sub-bass; spacious arrangements with reverb and delay; minor-key progressions dominate; piano and synth pads provide harmonic bed; production is polished but maintains street-adjacent edge
- **Energy/Mood**: Nocturnal, intimate, sensual, melancholic, vulnerable, cool; late-night mood music; emotionally open but retaining composure; heartbreak and desire as primary emotional territories; introspective without being self-pitying; confidence and vulnerability coexisting
- **Structure**: Verse-chorus with pre-chorus builds; 8-bar hooks common; verses may alternate between sung and rapped delivery; bridges often feature emotional peaks or vocal showcases; outros may dissolve into atmospheric production; standard pop-R&B song lengths (3-4 minutes)
- **Tempo**: Typically 60-80 BPM for downtempo ballads; mid-tempo tracks at 90-110 BPM; some uptempo tracks reach 120-130 BPM with half-time feel; the slower end of the spectrum dominates, creating intimate, bedroom atmosphere

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: AABB and ABAB — R&B-standard schemes; emphasis on melodic delivery over rhyme complexity; near rhymes and melodic resolution matter more than strict rhyme precision
- **Rhyme quality**: Simple to moderate; end rhymes serve the melody; internal rhymes present but not required; the vocal melody carries the song more than lyrical craft; conversational phrasing valued over technical construction
- **Verse structure**: 8-12 bars per verse; pre-choruses of 4 bars common; verses may blend singing and rapping; ad-libs and vocal runs fill space between phrases; some tracks use talk-singing in verses with fully sung choruses
- **Key rule**: Emotional specificity sells — vague love songs fail in trap soul; the best tracks name specific situations (late-night texts, driving alone, the moment before leaving) that create vivid emotional scenes
- **Avoid**: Over-singing or excessive melisma that overwhelms the production's space; generic love-song platitudes without specificity; aggressive or hard-edged delivery that breaks the intimate mood (unless deliberately contrasting); overly complex vocabulary that sounds unnatural in conversational singing
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **6 lines/verse** at 65-80 BPM. Slow tempos mean Suno has room — but melodic singing needs breathing space. At 90-110 BPM: 6-8 lines. Ballads under 65 BPM: max 4-6 lines for emotional weight. Topics: 1-2/verse.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Classic Trap Soul** | The Bryson Tiller blueprint: intimate vocals alternating between singing and rapping over 808-driven production; conversational delivery, relationship narratives, polished but not overproduced; the genre's core sound | Bryson Tiller, Tory Lanez, Jacquees |
| **Dark R&B / PBR&B** | The moodier, more atmospheric variant drawing from The Weeknd's influence; darker production, minor-key emphasis, themes of hedonism and emotional numbness; blurs into alternative R&B | The Weeknd, 6LACK, dvsn, PARTYNEXTDOOR |
| **Neo-Soul Trap** | Trap production merged with neo-soul vocal warmth and harmonic richness; more musically complex, gospel and jazz vocal influences, positive and empowering themes alongside vulnerability | H.E.R., Kehlani, SZA, Snoh Aalegra |
| **Minimalist Trap Soul** | Stripped-down production emphasizing space, silence, and bass; sparse arrangements with vocals carrying the weight; the most intimate variant; whispered and breathy delivery dominant | 6LACK, Brent Faiyaz, Summer Walker |
| **Aggressive Trap Soul** | Harder-edged production bridging trap soul with mainstream hip-hop; more confident and assertive vocal delivery; street themes mixed with relationship content; the most rap-adjacent variant | Tory Lanez, PartyNextDoor, Ty Dolla $ign |
| **Female Trap Soul** | Female-voiced variant with distinct emotional register; empowerment alongside vulnerability; more melodic range and vocal showcase moments; has produced some of the genre's biggest commercial successes | Summer Walker, SZA, H.E.R., Kehlani, Jhene Aiko |
| **Bedroom R&B** | DIY-produced variant emphasizing lo-fi warmth and extreme intimacy; soft-spoken delivery, minimal production, confessional tone; SoundCloud and bedroom-producer aesthetic | Brent Faiyaz (early work), Giveon, Frank Ocean (adjacent) |

## Reference Tracks

- **Bryson Tiller - "Don't"** — The track that launched trap soul: Tiller's conversational rap-singing over moody 808 production, a relationship narrative delivered with equal parts vulnerability and confidence; the song went viral on SoundCloud before RCA signed him, and its blueprint defined the genre

- **Bryson Tiller - "Exchange"** — *T R A P S O U L*'s emotional peak: more melodic than "Don't," with Tiller's singing carrying genuine heartbreak over warm 808 production; demonstrated the genre could deliver real emotional weight, not just vibes

- **The Weeknd - "Wicked Games"** — *House of Balloons*' centerpiece: dark, atmospheric production, Abel Tesfaye's falsetto carrying themes of hedonism and emptiness; predates the trap soul label but established the dark-R&B-over-electronic-production template the genre inherited

- **6LACK - "PRBLMS"** — Minimalist trap soul perfection: sparse 808 production, 6LACK's understated delivery, a relationship narrative told with cool emotional restraint; proved that space and silence could be as powerful as vocal pyrotechnics

- **SZA - "Love Galore" (feat. Travis Scott)** — *Ctrl*'s lead single: trap production meets neo-soul vocal warmth, SZA's confessional lyrics about toxic relationships, Travis Scott's atmospheric verse; bridged trap soul with a broader alternative R&B audience

- **Summer Walker - "Playing Games"** — *Over It*'s breakout: London on da Track production, Summer's breathy intimacy over dark 808 patterns; the track that made her the voice of trap soul's second generation

- **PARTYNEXTDOOR - "Recognize" (feat. Drake)** — OVO trap soul: moody Toronto production, Party's melodic vocal approach that influenced the entire genre, Drake's verse confirming the style's mainstream viability; early blueprint for the sound

- **Brent Faiyaz - "Gravity" (feat. Tyler, the Creator)** — *Wasteland*'s standout: Brent's effortless cool over lush production, Tyler's verse adding texture; demonstrates trap soul's maturation into a sophisticated, album-oriented art form

- **H.E.R. - "Focus"** — Neo-soul trap synthesis: guitar-playing singer over 808-driven production, vocal sophistication meeting modern beats; showed trap soul could incorporate musicianship without losing its street-adjacent edge

- **dvsn - "Hallucinations"** — OVO's purest trap soul: Daniel Daley's soaring vocals over Nineteen85's production, emotionally intense without being overwrought; demonstrates the Toronto sound's R&B depth

- **Jhene Aiko - "The Worst"** — Ethereal trap soul: whispered delivery over minimal production, emotional intimacy at maximum; Jhene's meditative approach showed the genre's gentlest extreme

- **Giveon - "Heartbreak Anniversary"** — Deep baritone over trap-soul production: proved the genre could accommodate different vocal textures beyond falsetto and auto-tune; retro warmth in a modern framework
