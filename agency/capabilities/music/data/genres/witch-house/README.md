# Witch House

## Genre Overview

Witch house emerged around 2009-2010 from the intersection of lo-fi electronic production, hip-hop beats, and occult-tinged visual aesthetics, coalescing on blogs and Tumblr before it had a stable name. Producers like oOoOO, Salem, and White Ring created murky, slowed-down electronic music that drew on chopped and screwed hip-hop, shoegaze, industrial, and dark ambient, wrapping it in deliberately obscure visual branding — inverted crosses, unicode symbols in artist names, and grainy black-and-white imagery. The genre was as much a visual and cultural movement as a sonic one.

Sonically, witch house is defined by its oppressive atmosphere and deliberate lo-fi degradation. Beats are slowed to a crawl (typically 80-100 BPM or slower), synths and samples are buried under layers of reverb and distortion, and vocals — when present — are pitched down, filtered, or otherwise obscured until they become ghostly textures. The production aesthetic favors darkness, weight, and claustrophobia: bass frequencies are heavy and distorted, treble is muffled or absent, and the overall sonic picture evokes VHS tape decay, occult rituals, and nocturnal paranoia. It sounds like pop music's corpse reanimated through dark magic.

The genre's peak cultural moment was brief — roughly 2010-2013 — but its influence proved lasting. Witch house's aesthetic DNA fed into the broader dark electronic underground, influencing artists across industrial, darkwave, and experimental electronic music. Crystal Castles occupied adjacent territory, while the genre's lo-fi production philosophy and occult aesthetics anticipated later movements in vaporwave and dark ambient. Labels like Tri Angle Records (not exclusively witch house but genre-adjacent) and numerous Bandcamp operations kept the sound alive. Modern witch house continues as a niche but active underground style, its atmosphere and production approach remaining distinctive.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Heavily distorted drum machines (808 patterns slowed down), dark synth pads, pitch-shifted samples (often from pop or R&B sources), sub-bass drones, industrial noise textures, tape-degraded loops, reversed sounds, horror movie samples

- **Vocals**: Pitched down and heavily processed; buried in reverb and distortion; often female vocals made spectral through processing; whispered or murmured delivery; sometimes unintelligible by design; chopped and screwed vocal techniques; when male, typically deep and droning

- **Production**: Lo-fi as aesthetic choice; heavy reverb and distortion on all elements; slowed-down tempos (chopped and screwed influence); tape saturation and degradation; bass-heavy with rolled-off high frequencies; dark, muffled mixing; mastering preserves murkiness rather than seeking clarity

- **Energy/Mood**: Occult, paranoid, nocturnal, oppressive; claustrophobic and disorienting; dark sexuality; ritualistic; the feeling of watching a horror film through static; simultaneously seductive and threatening

- **Structure**: Slow, hypnotic loops; minimal development; verse-like sections with vocal hooks buried in processing; builds through layering distortion and noise rather than traditional arrangement; 3-5 minutes typical; some tracks are essentially dark ambient with beats

- **Tempo**: 60-100 BPM (often slowed-down hip-hop or house tempos)

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: Free or none — lyrics are obscured by processing and serve atmosphere over meaning
- **Rhyme quality**: Textural; words chosen for how they sound when pitched down and drowned in reverb; meaning secondary to mood
- **Verse structure**: 2-4 lines per section; sparse, repetitive, mantra-like; often a single phrase looped
- **Key rule**: Write for obliteration. Lyrics will be pitched down, distorted, and buried — so prioritize evocative single phrases over narrative. Dark, occult, or sexually charged imagery. The words should feel like they are being heard through a wall.
- **Avoid**: Clarity; upbeat themes; complex storytelling; anything that requires intelligible delivery; cheerful or hopeful imagery
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **2-3 lines/verse** at 60-100 BPM. Sparse, slow, droning delivery. Heavily processed vocals. Topics: 1/track. Occult, dark desire, paranoia, nocturnal dread. Atmosphere is everything.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Classic Witch House** | Original blog-era sound: slowed beats, occult aesthetics, lo-fi murk | Salem, oOoOO, White Ring |
| **Drag** | Heavier, noisier variant with industrial and power electronics influences; more aggressive | Sidewalks and Skeletons, various Bandcamp artists |
| **Ethereal Witch House** | Lighter touch with shoegaze and dream pop influences; still dark but more melodic | ic3peak (adjacent), Crim3s |
| **Dark Club** | Modern evolution blending witch house atmosphere with dancefloor-functional tempos | Ritualz, various producers |

## Reference Tracks

- **Salem - "King Night"** — The genre's most visible statement: hip-hop beats slowed to a drugged crawl under distorted synths and buried vocals; confrontational, murky, and impossible to ignore

- **oOoOO - "Burnout Eyess"** — Pure witch house atmosphere: spectral vocals drifting over degraded beats and dark synths; lo-fi production as total aesthetic commitment; beautiful and deeply unsettling

- **White Ring - "Suffocation"** — Dark electronic pop filtered through witch house's distortion and pitch-shifting; melodic hooks barely visible through the murk; seductive and threatening

- **Sidewalks and Skeletons - "Goth"** — Heavier witch house with industrial weight: bass-heavy, distorted, and oppressive; demonstrates the genre's capacity for genuine sonic heaviness

- **Crystal Castles - "Baptism"** — Adjacent to witch house but sharing its DNA: noisy, aggressive, dark electronic with buried vocals and lo-fi distortion; Alice Glass's vocal treatment influential on the genre

- **Ritualz - "Ghetto Ass Witch"** — Club-functional witch house: maintains the dark atmosphere and processed vocals while adding enough rhythmic drive for dancefloor use; the genre's evolution toward accessibility
