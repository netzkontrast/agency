# HYPERPOP

## Genre Overview

Hyperpop is a maximalist experimental pop genre that emerged from online communities and the UK-based PC Music collective in the early-to-mid 2010s. Founded by A.G. Cook in 2013, PC Music became the aesthetic and philosophical nucleus of what would eventually be labeled "hyperpop"—a term popularized by Spotify's influential 2019 playlist of the same name. The collective's artists, including SOPHIE, Hannah Diamond, and Danny L Harle, established a sonic template that treated pop music as both subject and medium: deconstructing, exaggerating, and reconstructing commercial pop's tropes into something simultaneously celebratory and critical.

The genre is characterized by pitch-shifted vocals, distorted synthesizers, glitchy production techniques, and internet culture aesthetics. Hyperpop prioritizes creative excess and sonic chaos over commercial polish, blending elements from bubblegum pop, industrial, nightcore, trance, and avant-garde electronic music into a high-energy, often abrasive sound that challenges traditional pop conventions. Early hyperpop drew from the glossy digital textures of J-pop and K-pop, the speed and pitch manipulation of nightcore, the maximalism of EDM, and the conceptual playfulness of art-pop predecessors like Björk and Aphex Twin.

By the late 2010s, hyperpop had fractured into distinct scenes: the UK's PC Music axis maintained its conceptual art-pop focus; American artists like 100 gecs introduced elements of ska, metal, and country into the chaos; and a new generation of bedroom producers on SoundCloud and Discord created digicore—a lo-fi, emo-inflected variant that became TikTok's soundtrack. The tragic death of SOPHIE in 2021 marked a turning point, as the genre's most visionary figure was mourned while her influence continued to permeate mainstream pop through Charli XCX, Rina Sawayama, and countless others.

Hyperpop thrives on digital manipulation, unconventional song structures, and a DIY ethos rooted in online collaboration and distribution. Its aesthetic sensibility—maximalist, ironic, queer, and digitally native—reflects the experience of growing up online, where identity is fluid, culture is remixed endlessly, and the boundary between authentic emotion and performative excess becomes meaningless. The genre continues to evolve, influencing mainstream pop production while maintaining vibrant underground scenes across platforms from SoundCloud to Bandcamp.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Digital synthesizers (often heavily distorted or with aggressive LFO modulation), 808 and 909 drum machines, chiptune elements, vocal chops and pitch-shifting, glitchy electronic textures, trance leads, hardcore synth stabs, occasional live instruments filtered through extreme digital effects; bass design ranges from rubbery sub-bass to harsh distorted square waves

- **Vocals**: Pitch-shifted (often to extremes—falsetto, chipmunk-high, or bass-deep), heavily processed with formant shifting, multiple layered vocal lines creating dense harmonic textures, whispered or distorted passages, Auto-Tune as stylistic choice rather than correction, emphatic and expressive delivery, often gender-ambiguous or deliberately androgynous vocal presentation

- **Production**: Glitchy breakdowns with stuttered audio, abrupt transitions between sections, digital artifacts (clipping, aliasing, bitcrushing) as intentional aesthetic choices, hyper-compressed dynamics with extreme loudness, dense layering sometimes to the point of sensory overload, lo-fi or deliberately cheap-sounding elements juxtaposed with high-fidelity production, sidechain pumping as rhythmic element, trance gates, aggressive limiting

- **Energy/Mood**: Frenetic and chaotic, provocative, euphoric yet unsettling, playful but aggressive, often dark or ironic undertones paired with saccharine bubblegum sensibilities; emotional sincerity masked by or expressed through irony; themes of heartbreak, identity, internet alienation, and queer experience; simultaneously celebratory and critical of pop culture

- **Structure**: Non-traditional song structures with chaotic builds and sudden drops, unexpected key/tempo changes (sometimes mid-phrase), repetitive loops interrupted by jarring shifts, minimal verses with explosive choruses, genre-switching within single tracks, dramatic breakdowns, songs that feel like channel-surfing through multiple genres

## Lyric Conventions

- **Default rhyme scheme**: Loose — XAXA or free. Rules are deliberately broken for aesthetic effect.
- **Rhyme quality**: Playful, ironic, deliberately saccharine. Near rhymes and unconventional phrasing embraced.
- **Verse structure**: Short, punchy sections. Heavy vocal processing (pitch-shifting, distortion) affects how lyrics land.
- **Key rule**: Maximalist and genre-bending. Lyrics can be absurdist, ironic, or hyper-emotional. Vocal processing is part of the writing.
- **Avoid**: Conventional pop sincerity without ironic edge. Writing for unprocessed vocals when heavy effects are expected.
- **Density/pacing (Suno)**: Default **3 lines/verse** at 140-170 BPM. At 160+: 2-3 lines. Frenetic energy — minimal verses, hook-dominant. Fragment ideas; hyperpop is impressionistic. Topics: 1/verse.

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **PC Music / Art-Pop Hyperpop** | The original avant-garde pop variant from the PC Music collective; uses digital tools for unconventional pop songwriting with conceptual depth; treats pop as both medium and critique; polished production with sharp edges | A.G. Cook, SOPHIE, Hannah Diamond, Danny L Harle, Easyfun |
| **Bubblegum Bass** | Combines hyperpop's digital extremism with pop sensibility; sweet melodies and hooks over chaotic production; candy-coated but with bite; emphasizes contrast between saccharine and abrasive | Charli XCX, A.G. Cook, SOPHIE, Dorian Electra |
| **Glitchcore** | Emphasizes digital malfunction aesthetics; stuttering beats, codec errors, and audio glitches as primary musical elements; aggressive and chaotic; often incorporates elements from other genres (ska, metal, country) into the chaos | 100 gecs, Laura Les, dltzk |
| **Digicore** | Internet-native hyperpop blending lo-fi home recording with experimental pop sensibilities; emo and rap influences; often features pitched-up vocals over simple beats; emerged from Discord and SoundCloud communities; younger demographic | glaive, ericdoa, midwxst, p4rkr, brakence |
| **Deconstructed Club** | Experimental electronic music adjacent to hyperpop; deconstructs dance music into fractured, challenging forms; emphasizes texture and structure over melody; often instrumental; art gallery and fashion contexts | SOPHIE, Arca, Lotic, Amnesia Scanner |
| **Nightcore-Adjacent Hyperpop** | Draws directly from nightcore's sped-up, pitch-shifted aesthetic but with original compositions; uplifting trance influences; anime and gaming culture references; high BPM; ecstatic energy | Various SoundCloud producers, some 100 gecs tracks |
| **Emo Hyperpop / Glitchcore-Emo Fusion** | Combines hyperpop production with emo/pop-punk songwriting; themes of heartbreak and anxiety; pitched vocals over distorted guitars and 808s; nostalgic references to 2000s emo; confessional lyrics | glaive, midwxst, brakence, ericdoa |
| **Industrial Hyperpop** | Incorporates industrial and noise elements; harsher textures, mechanical rhythms, darker themes; less playful, more confrontational; influenced by acts like Death Grips and industrial EBM | SOPHIE (darker work), Arca, some Dorian Electra |

## Reference Tracks

- **SOPHIE - "Immaterial"** — The hyperpop thesis statement: impossibly processed vocals declaring freedom from physical form over euphoric synths; embodies the genre's queer, transcendent ethos; production is both harsh and ecstatic

- **SOPHIE - "Bipp"** — Early PC Music landmark that established the aesthetic: rubbery bass, metallic percussion, pitched vocals singing about desire; three minutes of perfectly engineered strangeness that sounds like nothing before it

- **100 gecs - "money machine"** — Pure glitchcore chaos: Laura Les's chipmunk vocals over Dylan Brady's ska-punk-dubstep production; absurdist lyrics about flex culture; the track that broke hyperpop into mainstream consciousness via meme culture

- **Charli XCX - "Vroom Vroom" (feat. SOPHIE)** — The mainstream breakthrough: Charli's pop star delivery over SOPHIE's most aggressive production; established the template for commercial hyperpop; the rev sounds became iconic

- **Charli XCX - "Track 10"** — From *Pop 2*; showcases hyperpop's emotional capacity: vulnerable lyrics about relationship anxiety over crystalline A.G. Cook production; proves the genre can be both experimental and genuinely moving

- **A.G. Cook - "Xxoplex"** — PC Music manifesto track: warped vocal samples, trance breakdowns, and a sense of joyful chaos; demonstrates how hyperpop deconstructs and celebrates dance music simultaneously

- **glaive - "astrid"** — Digicore essential: pitched-up vocals over simple guitar and 808s; emo songwriting meets hyperpop production; the sound of a generation making music in their bedrooms; raw and genuine despite processing

- **Danny L Harle - "On a Mountain"** — Happy hardcore hyperpop: euphoric vocals, trance arpeggios, hands-in-the-air energy; from the *Harlecore* virtual rave project; shows hyperpop's debt to 90s rave culture

- **Dorian Electra - "Gentleman"** — Theatrical hyperpop exploring masculinity: orchestral samples crash into distorted beats; visual album track with conceptual depth; demonstrates hyperpop's capacity for cultural commentary

- **Caroline Polachek - "So Hot You're Hurting My Feelings"** — Art-pop hyperpop crossover: sophisticated songwriting with experimental production touches; shows how hyperpop aesthetics can enhance rather than overwhelm traditional pop craft

- **Laura Les - "Haunted"** — Solo showcase: darker, more personal than 100 gecs; glitched vocals processing trauma into sound; demonstrates how hyperpop's digital manipulation can express emotional states words cannot

- **ericdoa & glaive - "sick of u"** — Digicore collaboration defining the sound: dual pitched vocals trading lines about heartbreak; simple beat construction with emotional maximalism; TikTok-era hyperpop distilled

- **underscores - "Locals (Girls Like Us)"** — Narrative hyperpop: storytelling about small-town queer experience over production that shifts between tender and chaotic; shows the genre's potential for concept albums

- **Arca - "Nonbinary"** — Deconstructed hyperpop: fractured beats, processed vocals exploring identity, production that refuses to settle; challenging but emotionally resonant; pushes hyperpop toward avant-garde territory

- **Hannah Diamond - "Every Night"** — PC Music emotional core: synth-pop hooks with crystalline production; genuine longing expressed through digital sheen; proves hyperpop can achieve classic pop songwriting heights while remaining sonically adventurous
