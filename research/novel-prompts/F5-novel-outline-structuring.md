> **Audience:** Gemini Deep Research (or equivalent).

## Question

What are the **canonical methodologies for structuring a novel OUTLINE**
— the phase AFTER concept-shaping (see F4) and BEFORE drafting — and
how do practitioners in 2026 actually structure scene-by-scene plans?

A "novel outline" is the bridge from a workable concept to a scene-
ready map. It answers: "Given that I know what this novel is ABOUT
(concept), what HAPPENS, in what ORDER, in which SCENES?"

Surface the **structured approaches** practitioners use, comparing
them across:

- **Granularity** — outlines vary from 5-paragraph synopses to 200-
  scene spreadsheets. What's the right grain for what purpose?
- **Artefact format** — beat sheets, scene cards, chapter outlines,
  pixel-grid plot diagrams, treatment documents.
- **Order of operations** — outline-in-order vs. outline-in-passes
  vs. tentpole-first vs. ending-first.
- **Revision discipline** — when is the outline "done"? When can you
  start drafting? When do you UPDATE the outline mid-draft?
- **Hand-off to drafting** — what does the outline produce as input
  to the drafting phase?

## What Gemini needs to cover

### A. The outline-grain spectrum

Outlines vary by granularity:

- **Synopsis** (1–5 pages) — paragraph-level summary of the whole
  novel. Often agent-submission format.
- **Treatment** (10–30 pages) — Hollywood-derived; chapter-by-chapter
  prose summary.
- **Beat sheet** (15–40 named beats) — the structural anchors only;
  Save-the-Cat 15 beats; Story Grid's 5 commandments; the Hero's
  Journey 12 stages.
- **Chapter outline** (20–50 chapters, one paragraph each) — the
  workhorse middle-grain outline.
- **Scene list** (60–200 scenes, one paragraph or card each) — the
  drafting-ready outline.
- **Beat-card outlines** (scenes as physical or digital index cards)
  — corkboard / Scrivener / Plottr workflows.
- **Mind-map / non-linear outlines** — Mindly, Scapple, hand-drawn
  diagrams.

When does each grain win? When is each grain wrong-for-purpose?

### B. The major outlining methodologies

For each, describe the artefact shape + the production process:

- **Save the Cat! Writes a Novel** (Brody) — the 15-beat sheet
  populated for the novel; then each beat expanded into the scenes
  that fulfil it.
- **Story Grid** (Coyne) — global story arc, then 5 commandments per
  unit (book / act / sequence / scene / beat), then the scene-by-
  scene "foolscap" worksheet.
- **The Snowflake Method** (Ingermanson) — step 6: expand each
  one-paragraph character summary to a one-page synopsis; step 7:
  expand the four-page synopsis to a scene-by-scene list (the actual
  outline).
- **K.M. Weiland's outlining method** — the General Sketch, then
  the Detailed Outline, then the Scene Outline.
- **John Truby's 22-step + scene-weave** — the 22 steps produce a
  beat list; then a scene-weave that maps subplots.
- **Larry Brooks's "Story Engineering"** — the four-part structure
  (setup, response, attack, resolution) with pinch points and
  midpoint; the chapter-by-chapter expansion.
- **Mary Robinette Kowal's writing method** — the MICE quotient
  (Milieu / Inquiry / Character / Event) outline; nested MICE
  threads; the close-of-each-thread sequencing.
- **The Dan Wells 7-point story structure** — hook, plot turn 1,
  pinch 1, midpoint, pinch 2, plot turn 2, resolution; expanded into
  scene outline.
- **The tentpole method** — name the 5–10 tentpole scenes first
  (climax, midpoint, opening, character-arc-pivot), then fill between.
- **The reverse-outline method** — start with the ending, work
  backwards through cause-and-effect.
- **The pure-scene-card method** — write each scene's purpose +
  conflict + outcome on a card; arrange and rearrange; outline
  emerges from cards.
- **The "what if?" method** — start with a "what if?" premise,
  outline the chain of consequences.
- **Drafting from beats only** (the discovery-writer compromise) —
  outline ONLY the 5–8 tentpoles; discover-write between.

### C. The outline-as-living-document discipline

When and how do practitioners update the outline mid-draft?

- **The "outline is sacred" school** — once approved, the outline
  drives draft; updates only between drafts.
- **The "outline is a hypothesis" school** — outline updates as
  drafting reveals what the story actually wants.
- **The "outline by chapter" school** — outline 3 chapters ahead of
  the draft; let discoveries reshape the next 3.
- **The discovery-writer's "no outline" school** — Stephen King; the
  outline EMERGES from drafting and is captured retroactively.
- **The "two outlines" school** — pre-draft outline (the plan) and
  post-draft outline (the actual structure) — used for revision.

### D. Outline-to-draft hand-off

What does each methodology hand off TO the drafting phase?

- **Scene cards / scene-list with goal-conflict-disaster** (Swain's
  scene/sequel; the Dwight Swain trichotomy).
- **Chapter outlines with point-of-view + setting markers**.
- **Beat sheets with emotional-anchor notes per beat**.
- **Just-in-time outlining** — outline the next scene right before
  drafting it (NaNoWriMo discovery-writers; Mary Robinette Kowal
  approach to short fiction).

### E. The pacing and tension axes

How outlines encode pacing:

- **Tension curves** — explicit Y-axis tension scores per scene; the
  tentpole-method's tension structure.
- **Subplot interleaving** — A-plot / B-plot / C-plot lane diagrams.
- **Scene density** — high-density (lots of plot per scene) vs.
  low-density (room for character / atmosphere).
- **Chapter cliffhangers** — when and how to end chapters for pull.

### F. The 2024–2026 evolution

- **AI-assisted outlining** — beat-sheet auto-generation; logline-to-
  outline expansion; scene-card auto-population (cite 2024+ tools;
  cross-reference F3).
- **Tool ecosystem 2024+** — Plottr, Scrivener, Campfire, World
  Anvil, Save-the-Cat Software, NovelCrafter outlining modules — which
  shapes do they push?
- **The serialised-fiction outlining wave** — Wattpad, Royal Road,
  Kindle Vella, Substack-fiction — different outline shapes for
  weekly-chapter serials vs. complete novels.
- **The "no outline" pushback** — discovery writers' response to
  AI-outliner pressure; the "let the AI outline, you discovery-write"
  hybrid.

## Anti-patterns (Gemini should avoid)

- Conflating outlining with concept-shaping. F4 covers concept; F5
  covers outline. The cutoff: concept ends with "I know what this
  novel is about and who's in it"; outlining begins with "what
  HAPPENS, in what scenes, in what order?".
- Conflating outlining with drafting. The Snowflake's step 7 is
  outlining; step 9 (the draft itself) is drafting.
- Recommending one outline shape as universally best. Surveys are
  descriptive; the §G recommendations are archetype-conditional.
- Skipping the discovery-writers' perspective. The "no outline"
  school is a valid methodology choice; surface it honestly.
- Treating outline length as quality. A 1-page synopsis can be a
  better outline than a 60-page treatment for some projects.
- Ignoring the serialised-fiction outlining wave. Wattpad / Royal
  Road / Vella / Substack serial fiction has its own outlining
  patterns (often closer to TV-writers' room methodology).
- Treating Scrivener as if it's a methodology. Scrivener is a tool
  that supports multiple methodologies; the methodology is what the
  author brings.
- Glossing the "outline as living document" question. Mid-draft
  outline updates are the most-divisive practical question; the
  schools genuinely conflict.
